# Ketone Measurement

## Claim
Blood beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) is the most accurate marker of nutritional ketosis. Urine acetoacetate strips work in the first weeks but lose sensitivity after adaptation. Breath acetone is reasonable and non-invasive but more variable. For most users, periodic blood BHB readings are sufficient — continuous measurement is not necessary.

## The three ketone bodies
- **Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB)** — the major circulating ketone; measured by blood meter.
- **Acetoacetate (AcAc)** — excreted in urine; measured by dipstick.
- **Acetone** — volatile byproduct; measured by breath analyzer.

All three rise and fall together, but they're not perfectly correlated.

## What the numbers mean (blood BHB)

| BHB (mmol/L) | State |
|--------------|-------|
| <0.2         | Not in ketosis |
| 0.2–0.5      | Light / adaptation |
| 0.5–3.0      | Nutritional ketosis |
| 3.0–5.0      | Therapeutic / deep ketosis (typical of classical therapeutic keto) |
| >5.0         | Uncommon in diet-only settings; investigate (prolonged fast, illness, or in T1D: DKA risk) |
| >10–15       | Diabetic ketoacidosis territory — emergency in appropriate clinical context |

**Nutritional ketosis ≠ ketoacidosis.** In non-T1D adults with intact insulin response, BHB is tightly regulated and cannot reach DKA levels from diet alone.

## Evidence
- **Laffel L. (Diabetes Metab Res Rev 1999)** — classic review of ketone body metabolism, measurement, and clinical interpretation.
- **Urbain & Bertz (Nutrients 2016)** — validation of breath acetone vs blood BHB during dietary ketosis.
- **Volek & Phinney (2011)** — applied framework.
- **Newman & Verdin (Annu Rev Nutr 2017) — β-hydroxybutyrate: a signaling metabolite.** Context beyond just "fuel."

## Practical guidance

- **Frequency:** Once or twice a week is enough once the user is established. Daily only during adaptation or when troubleshooting.
- **Timing:** Morning fasted reads the overnight state. Post-meal reads the acute insulin response.
- **Don't chase higher numbers.** BHB of 0.5 and BHB of 2.5 don't meaningfully differ in metabolic benefit for non-therapeutic goals.
- **Meter recommendations:** blood meters with BHB strips (e.g. Keto-Mojo, Precision Xtra). Avoid urine strips for post-adaptation monitoring.

## When to flag

- Persistent BHB <0.2 despite compliance with macros → re-check hidden carbs, protein load, or alcohol.
- BHB >5.0 in a well-adult without fasting context → investigate before continuing.
- In T1D or T2D on SGLT2/insulin: any unexplained BHB elevation + symptoms (nausea, abdominal pain, rapid breathing) → **urgent clinical evaluation for DKA**, regardless of glucose.

## Cost/benefit

For most metabolic-health or body-comp users, the signal-to-cost ratio of daily measurement is poor. The meaningful signals — weight, energy, glucose response, lipid panel at 8 weeks — move slower than daily ketone values and matter more.
