# Keto Macro Framework

## Claim
Nutritional ketosis is generally achieved at sustained net carbohydrate intake below ~50 g/day, with most individuals reaching it at 20–30 g/day. Protein requirements vary by goal and lean body mass. Fat fills the remaining caloric need. The *therapeutic* ketogenic diet (e.g. for epilepsy) uses stricter ratios and is distinct from *metabolic-health* and *body-composition* keto.

## Distinguishing the variants

| Variant | Net carbs | Protein | Fat | Typical use |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----|-------------|
| Therapeutic (classical 4:1 ratio) | <20 g | Minimum for needs | Very high | Drug-resistant epilepsy, under neurology direction |
| Modified Atkins (MAD) | 10–20 g | Moderate | High | Adult epilepsy, some neurological conditions |
| Nutritional (metabolic-health) | 20–50 g | Adequate-to-high | Fill remainder | Glycemic control, lipid panel improvement, satiety |
| Targeted / Cyclical | Higher around training or cycle days | High | Variable | Athletes, physique-focused |

## Evidence
- **Volek & Phinney — The Art and Science of Low Carbohydrate Living (2011) and Performance (2012).** Applied framework for nutritional ketosis in adults.
- **Hallberg et al. (Diabetes Therapy 2018) — 1-year Virta Health trial.** Sustained nutritional ketosis + remote physician support achieved meaningful A1c reduction and medication de-escalation in T2D.
- **Kossoff et al. (Epilepsia Open 2018) — Optimal clinical management of children receiving dietary therapies for epilepsy.** International consensus on therapeutic ketogenic therapy.
- **Bueno et al. (Br J Nutr 2013) — Meta-analysis of very-low-carbohydrate ketogenic vs low-fat diets** for weight loss. Modest advantage for very-low-carb in long-term studies.
- **Athinarayanan et al. (Frontiers Endocrinology 2019) — 2-year Virta follow-up.** Persistence of metabolic improvements with sustained adherence.

## Protein targets (nutritional keto)

Protein is not the enemy. Excess protein does not meaningfully exit ketosis in most adults with adequate carbohydrate restriction (gluconeogenesis is demand-driven, not substrate-driven, in the short term).

- **Sedentary maintenance:** 0.6–0.8 g / lb lean body mass
- **Body-composition / resistance-training:** 0.8–1.0 g / lb LBM; up to 1.2 g / lb during a deficit to preserve lean mass
- **Older adults / sarcopenia-prevention:** 1.0+ g / lb LBM

## Fat as the remainder

Fat quality matters for cardiovascular markers. Emphasize:
- Monounsaturated (olive, avocado)
- Polyunsaturated from whole-food sources (fatty fish, nuts/seeds in carb budget)
- Moderate saturated — individual response varies (see LDL/ApoB monitoring below)

Limit:
- Industrial seed oils in large quantities
- Ultra-processed "keto" products

## Monitoring schedule

- **Weight:** weekly, same conditions.
- **Ketones:** blood BHB is the accurate marker. 0.5–3.0 mmol/L is nutritional range. Urine strips lose sensitivity after adaptation. Breath acetone is reasonable but variable.
- **Labs baseline:** fasting glucose, A1c, insulin, lipid panel (including ApoB), uric acid, TSH, CBC, CMP.
- **Labs follow-up:** 8–12 weeks in, then 6-monthly.
- **Watch specifically:** ApoB/LDL-C trajectory — a minority of people ("lean mass hyper-responders") show pronounced LDL elevation on saturated-fat-heavy keto. If ApoB rises >20% from baseline, modify fat sources or escalate to clinician.

## Who this does NOT apply to
- Type 1 diabetes without endocrinologist-supervised adaptation (DKA risk).
- Pregnancy / lactation.
- History of eating disorders (restriction can be destabilizing).
- Gallbladder disease / pancreatitis history.
- Medications requiring carbohydrate stability: **insulin, sulfonylureas, SGLT2 inhibitors** (SGLT2 + keto = elevated DKA risk even in T2D — often contraindicated).
- Porphyria, defects in fat metabolism (primary carnitine deficiency, MCAD, etc.).
- Renal impairment without nephrologist guidance.

## Adaptation timeline

- **Days 1–4:** glycogen depletion, water loss, possible "keto flu" (mostly electrolyte-driven).
- **Weeks 1–3:** metabolic adaptation. Exercise performance can dip then recover.
- **Weeks 4–8:** steady-state. Most people re-equilibrate appetite and energy.
- **8+ weeks:** first meaningful lab checkpoint.
