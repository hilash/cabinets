# Keto Contraindications & Precautions

## Claim
A ketogenic diet is **not appropriate** for some individuals, and requires clinician supervision for others. Screen before starting. If any of the absolute contraindications below apply, do not proceed without medical direction.

## Absolute contraindications (do not proceed on an informational-agent basis)

- **Type 1 diabetes** without endocrinology supervision — DKA risk.
- **Pregnancy and lactation** — inadequate evidence for safety; avoid.
- **Primary carnitine deficiency, carnitine palmitoyltransferase I or II deficiency, carnitine translocase deficiency.**
- **Fatty acid oxidation disorders** (MCAD, LCAD, VLCAD, long-chain 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase deficiency, medium-chain 3-ketoacyl thiolase deficiency).
- **Pyruvate carboxylase deficiency.**
- **Porphyria.**

Source: **Kossoff et al. (Epilepsia Open 2018) — Optimal clinical management of children receiving dietary therapies for epilepsy.** Defines the formal contraindication list that applies equally in adult contexts.

## Relative contraindications / requires physician direction

- **Type 2 diabetes on insulin, sulfonylureas, meglitinides, or SGLT2 inhibitors** — hypoglycemia and euglycemic DKA risk. Medications will need adjustment.
- **Hypertension on medications** — blood pressure often drops with keto; medications need retitration.
- **Heart failure** — electrolyte management is clinical-level.
- **Chronic kidney disease (eGFR <60)** — protein, electrolyte, and hydration management changes.
- **History of gallbladder disease or pancreatitis** — high-fat intake can precipitate symptoms.
- **History of eating disorders** (AN, BN, orthorexia) — restriction-based protocols can be destabilizing.
- **Children and adolescents** — not appropriate except under pediatric clinical supervision for specific indications.
- **Severe liver dysfunction.**
- **Familial hypercholesterolemia or existing high LDL/ApoB** — saturated-fat sources need careful selection with lipid monitoring.

## Medication interactions to flag before starting

| Medication class | Concern |
|-----------------|---------|
| Insulin | Hypoglycemia — dose reduction required |
| Sulfonylureas / meglitinides | Hypoglycemia |
| SGLT2 inhibitors | Euglycemic DKA risk — often discontinued for keto |
| Metformin | Generally compatible but review with prescriber |
| Antihypertensives | BP may drop — retitration likely |
| Diuretics | Interact with electrolyte shifts; possible dose change |
| Lithium | Sodium flux affects lithium levels — monitor |
| Anticonvulsants (non-keto-indicated) | Some affect carnitine status — review |
| Statins | Lipid panel interpretation changes; typically continued |

## Screening questions before the first brief

The Keto Coach should confirm on first run:
1. Are you pregnant, trying to conceive, or breastfeeding? [if yes: stop]
2. Do you have type 1 diabetes? [if yes: defer]
3. Do you take insulin, sulfonylureas, or an SGLT2 inhibitor? [if yes: defer until prescriber consulted]
4. Do you take blood pressure medications? [if yes: flag for BP monitoring + prescriber]
5. Any history of an eating disorder? [if yes: discuss with clinician before proceeding]
6. Any gallbladder / pancreatic / liver / kidney disease? [if yes: defer]
7. Any known metabolic disorder (FAOD, carnitine deficiency, porphyria)? [if yes: stop]

If any "defer" or "stop": Keto HQ writes a short note to the user, pauses automated briefs, and recommends a clinician conversation before activating.

## When to stop an active protocol

- New abdominal pain, persistent nausea, or right-upper-quadrant pain — rule out gallbladder/pancreas.
- Unexplained fatigue, weakness, or muscle pain lasting >2 weeks beyond adaptation window.
- Persistent heart palpitations despite electrolyte correction.
- Labs show ApoB/LDL-C rising sharply (>20% from baseline) on saturated-fat-heavy pattern.
- Menstrual irregularity lasting >2 cycles.
- Any DKA-suggestive symptoms in T2D on medication: nausea, abdominal pain, rapid breathing, fruity breath — **urgent evaluation**.

## Not medical advice

This file is a screening tool, not a replacement for medical evaluation. Complex medical histories require a clinician in the loop before starting and during the protocol.
