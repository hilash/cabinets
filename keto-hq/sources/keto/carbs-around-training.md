# Carbs Around Training

## Claim
For keto-adapted adults training at high intensity (anaerobic / glycolytic work — lifting, sprints, CrossFit-type sessions), a modest targeted carbohydrate intake (~15–30 g) in the 30–60 minutes before or during training can support output without meaningfully disrupting long-term metabolic adaptation. This is **optional** — strictly ketogenic training works for many, especially at lower-intensity / aerobic efforts.

## Who this is for
- Resistance trainees with heavy compound work.
- Sprint / interval athletes.
- Users reporting persistent drop in top-set performance after full adaptation (4+ weeks).

Lower-intensity athletes (zone-2 cardio, endurance < threshold) generally do not benefit from carb targeting — fat oxidation is adequate for the demand.

## Evidence
- **Volek et al. (Metabolism 2016) — FASTER study.** Keto-adapted ultra-endurance runners sustained similar performance as carb-adapted peers. Aerobic-domain evidence.
- **Burke LM (Sports Med 2015) — Re-examining high-fat diets for performance.** Balanced review; high-intensity glycolytic work is where LCHF shows consistent performance decrements vs high-carb.
- **Paoli et al. (Nutrients 2019) — Ketogenic diet and strength / power athletes.** Mixed results; body-comp benefits may trade against top-end power.
- **Escobar et al. (J Strength Cond Res 2016) — Low-dose pre-workout CHO in trained athletes.** Demonstrates performance benefit at small doses.

Evidence tier: **emerging** for the targeted approach. Good evidence that strict keto impairs high-intensity output; less robust evidence on the precise dose/timing that restores it without disrupting adaptation.

## Practical framework

**Dose:** 15–30 g of readily absorbable carbs.

**Timing:** 30–60 minutes pre-training, or sipped during a long session.

**Forms that work:**
- Dextrose / glucose tabs
- White rice (if time allows)
- Berries + a small amount of honey
- Banana (watch portion — a whole banana is ~25g carbs)

**Avoid:**
- Fructose-heavy sources (less effective for muscle glycogen replenishment)
- Sugar alcohols (GI risk under load)
- Ultra-processed "performance gels" with added fats/proteins (slow absorption)

## Integration with daily macros

- Count the pre-workout carbs in your daily net carb budget.
- On rest days, return to standard keto targets.
- **Cyclical keto** (weekend carb refeeds) is a different framework — see variants in [[macro-framework]].

## Who should not carb-target

- Therapeutic keto (epilepsy, specific neurological conditions) — adherence to strict ratios is the point.
- Newly keto (<4 weeks in) — let adaptation complete first.
- T2D with significant insulin resistance in active reversal — coordinate with clinician.

## Signals it's working

- Top-set performance returns to or near pre-adaptation baseline.
- Post-workout energy stable, not crash-prone.
- No disruption to overnight BHB or fasting glucose.

## Signals it's not working / stop

- Persistent glucose spikes or headaches post-workout.
- GI distress from source.
- Performance benefit not evident after 2 weeks of consistent targeting — may not be the missing variable.
