# Northbound Capital — Fund Thesis

> This is the document every agent scores and writes against. Edit it first to make this cabinet your own. The Deal Scout's scores, the Research Analyst's market reads, and the Platform Lead's daily threads all flow from here.

## In one line

We back technical founders building **applied-AI products and the developer infrastructure underneath them** at seed and Series A, where the wedge is a real workflow, not a demo.

## What we invest in

**Vertical 1 — Applied AI for a specific workflow.** Software that uses AI to own an end-to-end job-to-be-done in a vertical (legal, healthcare ops, finance, support, security), not a thin wrapper. We want products where AI changes the unit economics of the work, not just the UI.

**Vertical 2 — Developer & AI infrastructure.** The tools teams need to build, evaluate, deploy, and observe AI systems in production: eval and observability, orchestration, data/retrieval infra, inference optimization, agent tooling. We believe the picks-and-shovels layer is still being built.

**Vertical 3 — AI-native security.** Both AI *for* security (detection, response, posture) and security *for* AI (model, agent, and data-layer threats). New attack surface, new defenders.

## What we look for

- **Technical founders** who could build the first version themselves and have an unfair insight into the problem.
- **A real wedge** — a specific workflow or buyer where the product is already pulling, even pre-revenue (design partners using it weekly beats a waitlist).
- **A path to a durable moat** — proprietary data, workflow lock-in, or a distribution edge. "We're faster/cheaper" is not a moat.
- **Capital efficiency** — teams that can get to a strong Series A on a seed round, or a strong B on an A. We index hard on efficient growth in this market.

## What we pass on

- Thin LLM wrappers with no proprietary data or workflow ownership.
- Consumer social / creator tooling (not our edge).
- Hardware, biotech, and deep-science plays requiring long capital-intensive R&D before revenue.
- Markets we judge too small to return the fund (a 12% stake in a $100M outcome doesn't move an $80M fund).
- Founders we can't get conviction on, at any price.

## How we win

1. **Proprietary deal flow** from a public point of view in our verticals (the daily thread) and a strong founder network.
2. **Speed and clarity** — founders get a real answer fast, and a written reason on every pass.
3. **Platform value-add** — talent, customer intros, PR, and next-round positioning that make us worth more than our check.

## Fund mechanics (for scoring "round fit")

- **Stage:** Seed to Series A.
- **Initial check:** $1M–$4M.
- **Ownership target:** 10–15% at entry.
- **Reserves:** roughly 2:1 of initial check size for follow-on; we defend pro-rata in our winners.
- **Sane entry valuation:** seed up to ~$15M post; Series A up to ~$50M post. Above that, the math needs an exceptional story.
