# Quillstream — Diligence Brief

> **Bottom line:** Strong recommend to advance to a term sheet. Repeat fintech founder owning a painful, recurring, regulated workflow with proprietary data and real expansion. The one thing to get conviction on is entry valuation — it's at the top of our sane band.

## Snapshot

Quillstream is an AI agent that drafts and files regulatory submissions (state money-transmitter licenses, SAR/CTR filings, audit responses) for fintechs and neobanks. It turns a workflow that costs a fintech a compliance team and outside counsel into a reviewed-and-filed artifact in hours. **Series A, raising $12M on $48M post; we'd take ~$3M for ~10%.** (Vertical 1 — applied AI for a specific workflow.)

## Team

- **CEO — repeat founder.** Built and sold a payments-compliance tooling company to a top-5 US bank (2021). Knows the buyer, the regulators, and where the bodies are buried in the workflow.
- **CTO — ex-staff engineer** at a large neobank, owned their internal compliance automation. Founder-market fit is the strongest part of this deal.
- 14 people; hired ahead of revenue in eng, deliberately thin on sales (founder-led so far).

## Market

- US regulatory-compliance software for financial services: **~$12B, growing ~11%/yr** (third-party industry sizing; sanity-checked against two analyst reports). The AI-native slice is nascent.
- **Why now:** licensing and reporting burden is rising while fintechs are cutting compliance headcount — the cost curve and the AI capability curve crossed in the last 18 months.
- SAM the company can realistically own (US fintech + neobank + crypto-adjacent): we estimate **~$1.5–2B**, enough for a fund-returning outcome at our ownership.

## Competition

| Player | Position | Threat |
|---|---|---|
| Legacy GRC suites (incumbents) | Own the buyer, slow to add real AI | Distribution moat, but product is a generation behind |
| Horizontal AI-doc startups | Generic document automation | Lack the regulatory data + workflow depth |
| In-house + outside counsel | Status quo | The real competitor; Quillstream wins on cost + speed |

Differentiation: a **proprietary corpus of filings and regulator responses** that makes the agent's drafts pass review the first time. That data flywheel is the moat — confirm its defensibility in the partner call.

## Traction

- **ARR $3.1M, +11% MoM** (verified against Stripe export shared in the data room).
- **NRR 124%** — strong expansion as customers add filing types (verified).
- 28 customers; logo concentration acceptable (top customer ~14% of ARR).
- Gross margin 71% (some human-in-the-loop review cost; trending up as the model improves).

## Risks (how this goes to zero)

1. **Valuation/entry math.** $48M post is the top of our sane Series A band. The model only works if this is the last dilutive round before a clean B — pressure-test the plan to get to $10M+ ARR on this raise.
2. **Regulatory liability.** If an agent-drafted filing is materially wrong, who's liable? Need to see the review/QA architecture and the insurance/contractual posture.
3. **Incumbent wake-up.** A legacy GRC suite bolting on "good enough" AI could slow Quillstream's mid-market expansion. The data moat is the defense — confirm it's real and growing.

## Open questions for the founder

- What's the path to $10M ARR on this raise, and what's the hiring plan to get there?
- Walk us through the review architecture — how do you keep a wrong filing from going out?
- How proprietary is the filings corpus, and does it compound with each customer?
- Pro-rata: will you protect our ability to follow on into the B?
