# 🚀 Publishing Guide

Five real paths. Each has different cost, time, control, and prestige trade-offs. There is no objectively best path — there's the right path for the book + author + goals.

## The decision matrix

| Goal | Path |
|---|---|
| Fastest, lowest barrier, max control, Amazon-only | **[[kdp\|KDP]]** |
| Broader distribution including bookstores, still self-published | **[[ingramspark\|IngramSpark]]** (often paired with KDP) |
| Multi-platform self-publishing without managing each | **[[draft2digital\|Draft2Digital]]** |
| Some publisher support, faster than traditional, you co-fund | **[[hybrid\|Hybrid press]]** |
| Maximum prestige, broadest reach, traditional publishing | **[[traditional\|Agent + Big 5 / mid-size]]** |

## Quick comparison

| | KDP | IngramSpark | Draft2Digital | Hybrid | Traditional |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Time to market | Days | 2–4 weeks | 1–2 weeks | 6–18 months | 18–36 months |
| Upfront cost | $0 | ~$50 + cover/edit | $0 | $5,000–25,000 | $0 |
| Royalty % | 35–70% | ~40–55% | ~60–70% | varies | 8–15% |
| Bookstore distribution | Limited | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Prestige signal | Low | Low | Low | Mid | High |
| Marketing burden | 100% you | 100% you | 100% you | Mostly you | Shared (but mostly you) |

## The two questions to answer first

1. **Why this book?** Authority-building / lead-gen for a business / income / artistic / legacy. The "why" determines the right path. If it's lead-gen for a business, KDP + Amazon is enough. If it's a career-defining work, traditional may matter.

2. **What's the audience situation?** If you have 10K+ engaged audience members, self-publishing CAN match traditional reach. If you have <1K, traditional gives you access to discovery you can't match alone.

## The single most important strategic point

For most non-fiction first books — especially business / professional / how-to — **self-publishing is the right call**. You'll earn more money, ship faster, and own the rights.

For memoir, narrative non-fiction, or books seeking literary recognition — **traditional often makes more sense**. But the trade-off is speed and royalty.

## The marketing burden truth

Whatever path you pick: **you market the book**. Even traditional publishers mostly publish; they don't mostly market. Mid-list authors with traditional publishers do 90% of their own marketing. Plan accordingly.

## A typical self-publishing stack

The "self-publishing power combo" most successful indie authors use:

```
KDP            → Kindle ebook + Amazon paperback
IngramSpark    → Hardcover + bookstore distribution
Draft2Digital  → Apple Books, Kobo, Nook, Google Play
ACX            → Audiobook
Substack/Newsletter → Direct sales of book + ongoing reader relationship
```

This combo gets you everywhere readers buy books, with reasonable royalties, in 2–4 weeks of setup. You can match traditional distribution at a fraction of the time.
