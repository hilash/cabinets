# 🟧 KDP — Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing

> Amazon's self-publishing platform. ~80% of indie books sell on Amazon. KDP is non-negotiable. The only question is what you pair it with.

## Pick KDP if

- You want to publish self-published — KDP is the floor, not a choice
- Amazon is where your readers are (it usually is for non-fiction)
- You want fast time-to-market (days, not months)
- You don't mind being primarily on one platform

## Don't ONLY pick KDP if

- You want bookstore distribution → add IngramSpark
- You want to reach Apple Books / Kobo / Nook readers → add Draft2Digital
- You want hardcovers → KDP's hardcover is OK; IngramSpark's is better

## What KDP offers

- Kindle ebook
- Paperback (POD — Print on Demand)
- Hardcover (POD)
- Audiobook (via ACX, Amazon's audiobook arm — separate process)
- Translations marketplace
- Author Central page
- Kindle Direct deals + categories + keyword targeting

## Royalty rates

| Format | Rate |
|---|---|
| Kindle ebook (priced $2.99–$9.99) | 70% royalty (minus delivery fee) |
| Kindle ebook (other prices) | 35% |
| Paperback / hardcover | ~60% list minus printing cost |

Example: a $14.99 paperback prints for ~$4 → you net ~$5/book. For a 300-page book.

## KDP Select — the trade-off

KDP Select is opt-in. If you enroll:
- Your ebook is exclusive to Amazon for 90 days
- You get access to Kindle Unlimited (KU) — readers borrow your book and you earn per page read
- You get 5 free-promo days every 90 days

Trade-off: NO ebook on Apple Books / Kobo / Nook for those 90 days.

For most non-fiction first books: **opt INTO KDP Select for the first 90 days**, see how KU + free promo perform, then re-evaluate.

## Setup workflow

1. Create KDP account at kdp.amazon.com
2. Format the manuscript (Word, Vellum, Atticus, or Reedsy Studio)
3. Generate the cover (see `generators/cover-art-generator.md`) — KDP has cover specs and a calculator
4. Upload manuscript + cover
5. Set metadata: title, subtitle, author, description, keywords (7), categories (3), price
6. Order an author proof copy. Wait 4–7 days. Read every page. Errors will be there.
7. Approve. Live in 24–48 hours.

## The 7 keywords

These determine where Amazon shows your book in search. Use long-tail phrases, not single words. Tools to research: Publisher Rocket (paid), KDSPY, even Amazon's own search bar (type your topic, see what auto-completes).

## The categories

You're allowed up to 10 categories on KDP — but the form only shows 3. Email KDP support to request the additional 7. Niche down: ranking #1 in "Career Guides for Engineers" beats ranking #5,000 in "Business and Money".

## Common KDP mistakes

- Skipping the proof copy → typos and formatting errors live forever in customer reviews
- Bad categories → invisible in search
- Pricing too high or too low — for non-fiction in 2026, $14.99–$24.99 paperback / $4.99–$9.99 ebook is the sweet spot
- Missing the "look inside" optimization — the first 10% of the book MUST hook the reader, since that's what Amazon shows
- Forgetting to claim Author Central — your name on the spine + a profile + recent activity = a real signal
