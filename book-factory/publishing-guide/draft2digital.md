# 🌐 Draft2Digital

> The aggregator. Upload once, distribute everywhere except Amazon. Free, simple, and the path of least resistance for non-Amazon ebook distribution.

## Pick Draft2Digital if

- You want your ebook on Apple Books, Kobo, Barnes & Noble, Google Play, etc.
- You don't want to manage 5+ different platform accounts
- You're already on KDP and want to add non-Amazon distribution

## Don't pick Draft2Digital if

- You want bookstore / library physical distribution → IngramSpark
- You're enrolled in KDP Select (which requires Amazon ebook exclusivity for 90 days)

## Pricing

- Free to use
- They take ~10% of net royalty per sale
- No upfront cost

## Distribution channels

Draft2Digital distributes to:
- Apple Books
- Kobo (Rakuten)
- Barnes & Noble (Nook)
- Google Play
- Tolino (German market)
- Smashwords
- OverDrive (libraries)
- Vivlio
- Bibliotheca

Plus a few smaller channels. ~95% of non-Amazon ebook reading happens via these.

## Workflow

1. Sign up free at draft2digital.com
2. Upload your formatted ebook (EPUB) and cover
3. Set metadata + price
4. Pick which channels to distribute to
5. Hit publish

That's it. Your book is live on all selected channels in 24h–2 weeks (Apple is fastest; Tolino can be slow).

## Royalty rates

Vary by channel:
- Apple Books: 70% of list (D2D takes ~10%, so you net ~60%)
- Kobo: 70% of list (you net ~60%)
- Google Play: 70% (you net ~60%)
- Barnes & Noble: similar

Apple is typically your second-biggest non-Amazon channel for non-fiction ebooks — sometimes 10–20% of total ebook revenue.

## Why use Draft2Digital instead of going direct

You CAN sign up directly to each platform. But:
- Apple Books requires a Mac to upload directly
- Each platform has its own quirks, dashboards, payment thresholds
- D2D normalizes pricing, royalty reports, payments

The 10% fee buys you not having to manage 5+ vendor relationships. Most indie authors decide this is worth it.

## Smashwords

D2D acquired Smashwords in 2022. Smashwords used to be the alternative aggregator. Now they're the same company. For new authors: just use Draft2Digital.

## When to drop D2D

If one specific channel becomes very large for you (e.g. Kobo is 30% of your revenue), you might consider going direct to that channel and saving the 10%. Not worth doing for under $5K/year through any single channel.

## D2D Print

D2D recently added print-on-demand via Ingram's network. It's an alternative to using IngramSpark directly. Slightly easier setup, slightly fewer customization options. For new authors, IngramSpark direct is still the better choice — but D2D Print is worth watching as it improves.
