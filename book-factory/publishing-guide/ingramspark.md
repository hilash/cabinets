# 📦 IngramSpark

> Distribution into actual bookstores (Barnes & Noble, indies, libraries) — and a stronger hardcover offering than KDP. The complement to KDP, not a replacement.

## Pick IngramSpark if

- You want your book in actual brick-and-mortar bookstores
- You want libraries to be able to order it
- You want a higher-quality hardcover than KDP offers
- You're publishing a coffee-table / gift book (their printing options exceed KDP's)

## Don't ONLY pick IngramSpark

- KDP still owns most non-fiction sales — pair them, don't replace

## Pricing

- Ebook: free to upload
- Print: ~$49 setup fee per format (sometimes waived with promo codes via the Alliance of Independent Authors or NaNoWriMo) + ~$25 to update files later

## Distribution scope

IngramSpark distributes through Ingram, the largest book wholesaler in North America. That means:
- Available to order from any indie bookstore
- Listed in Barnes & Noble's system
- Available to libraries
- International distribution (UK, Australia, EU)
- ALSO ends up on Amazon (via wholesale channel) — but at a worse royalty than KDP

## The KDP + IngramSpark combo

Most successful indie authors do this:

| Format | Sold via |
|---|---|
| Kindle ebook | KDP only |
| Paperback (Amazon) | KDP — better royalty here |
| Paperback (everywhere else) | IngramSpark — broad distribution |
| Hardcover | IngramSpark — better quality + distribution |
| Audiobook | ACX (Amazon) + Findaway Voices (everywhere else) |

You list the same book on both platforms with separate ISBNs (or the same ISBN if you handle it carefully). Amazon's wholesale channel will source from KDP first, so you keep KDP's better royalty.

## ISBN strategy

KDP gives you a free ISBN (Amazon owns it — book is "published by Amazon"). IngramSpark also offers free ISBNs but with the same caveat.

For a serious self-publisher: buy your own ISBNs from Bowker (US) or your country's ISBN agency. ~$295 for 10. The book is then "published by [your imprint name]" — looks more professional, more flexibility down the road.

## Print quality

IngramSpark's print quality is meaningfully better than KDP's:
- Better paper options (cream, white, premium)
- Better cover finishes (matte, gloss, soft-touch)
- Better hardcover binding (smyth-sewn vs glued)
- True bookstore-grade trim sizes

For a "real" hardcover that looks publisher-grade, IngramSpark.

## Setup workflow

1. Create IngramSpark account
2. Buy your own ISBN (recommended) or use IngramSpark's free one
3. Format manuscript to print specs (different from KDP's specs — check IngramSpark's templates)
4. Upload manuscript + cover (cover specs vary by trim size + page count — use their calculator)
5. Order an author proof. Wait 1–2 weeks (longer than KDP). Review every page.
6. Approve. Distribution kicks in within 6–8 weeks for full bookstore visibility.

## Returns

IngramSpark allows bookstores to ORDER your book and RETURN it if it doesn't sell. "Returnable" books get more bookstore stock orders. But returnable books also mean you might owe IngramSpark for unsold copies (you've been paid for the wholesale order; if it's returned, the money reverses).

For first-time authors: enable returns on hardcovers (low risk; bookstores rarely take risks on unknown authors with returns OFF), but disable returns on paperbacks until you have track record.

## Most common IngramSpark mistake

Uploading a KDP-formatted cover. KDP and IngramSpark have different bleed and spine specs. The exact same book has DIFFERENT cover files for each platform. Don't reuse without re-checking.
