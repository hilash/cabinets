# 📚 Chapters

The book. Each chapter lives in its own folder under `chapters/<NNN-slug>/`:

| File | What it is |
|---|---|
| `draft.md` | The chapter draft |
| `notes.md` | Author notes — concerns, questions, marginalia |
| `index.md` | Chapter overview + status + revision passes |

## Folder convention

```
chapters/
  001-introduction/
    index.md
    draft.md
    notes.md
  002-<slug>/
    ...
```

## The chapter loop

Every chapter follows this shape — the writing equivalent of a working spine:

```
HOOK → STAKE → ARGUMENT/TEACHING → EVIDENCE → APPLICATION → BRIDGE
```

- **HOOK** — open on a story, a question, or a contradiction. Earn the first 200 words.
- **STAKE** — why does THIS chapter matter to the reader? What does it cost them not to know this?
- **ARGUMENT/TEACHING** — the spine. The actual content. The thing the chapter delivers.
- **EVIDENCE** — examples, anecdotes, citations, case studies. Mark unsourced as `[TK]`.
- **APPLICATION** — the "what to do with this" — concrete next move for the reader.
- **BRIDGE** — one paragraph linking to the next chapter. Earn the page-turn.

## Revision passes

Each chapter goes through three passes:

1. **Structural** — does the chapter deliver on its premise? Are sections in the right order? Is anything missing? Is anything extra?
2. **Line** — sentence-by-sentence. Voice consistency. Cut filler. Strengthen verbs.
3. **Polish** — grammar, rhythm, the final read-aloud test.

Don't do all three at once. Do structural first across all chapters before any line edit.
