## What an introduction has to do

The introduction is the highest-stakes chapter in the book. The reader has 5 minutes to decide whether to commit to 8+ hours of reading. The introduction must:

1. **State the premise** — clearly. The reader should know within the first 500 words what problem this book solves and whether they're the right reader.
2. **Earn the right to teach this** — establish credibility through specific experience, NOT generic credentials.
3. **Set the stakes** — what does it cost the reader not to know what's in this book?
4. **Promise the structure** — what kind of book is this (argument, narrative, how-to)? What will each chapter deliver?
5. **NOT teach yet** — save the actual teaching for chapter 2. The introduction is about commitment, not content.

The most common mistake first-time authors make: trying to teach in the introduction. Resist.
