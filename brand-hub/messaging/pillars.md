# Messaging Pillars

Last updated: 2026-05-23

## Pillar 1 — Speed without chaos
**Core claim:** Teams ship faster when their context travels with their work.
**Approved language:** "Ship faster," "no setup overhead," "your stack, intact," "in minutes, not weeks."
**Avoid:** "Revolutionary," "game-changing," "disruptive."

## Pillar 2 — Engineering-native
**Core claim:** Built for how engineering teams actually work — async, distributed, decision-heavy.
**Approved language:** "Built for teams that ship," "async-first," "engineering-native," "decision log," "no meetings required."
**Avoid:** "Enterprise-grade" without qualification, "for all teams" (we're specific), "Slack replacement."

## Pillar 3 — Your data, your rules
**Core claim:** No lock-in, no black box. Everything is a file you control.
**Approved language:** "Every artifact is a file," "export anything," "no lock-in," "EU data residency available," "open format."
**Avoid:** "Proprietary," "cloud-native only," "our AI handles it" without explaining the model.

## Brand voice
Tone: Opinionated, specific, direct. No filler adjectives. If you can't point at what makes the claim true, cut it.
