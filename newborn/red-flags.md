# 🚑 Red flags — when to call the doctor

> Print this. Stick it on the fridge. Your brain doesn't work at 3am, and that's exactly when you'll need it.

## CALL 911 / 112 / your local emergency number now

- Baby is **not breathing** or breathing is very fast / labored / has bluish lips
- Baby is **unconscious** or won't wake up
- Baby has a **seizure** or is unusually limp
- **Severe allergic reaction** — face/lip swelling + difficulty breathing
- Severe injury, head injury, burns, or bleeding that won't stop

## ER NOW for a baby under 3 months

- **Rectal temperature ≥ 100.4°F (38°C)** — this is the rule. Not the pediatrician — the ER. Under-3-month fever workup is non-negotiable.
- Forceful, repeated vomiting (not just spit-up — true vomiting that empties the stomach)
- Persistent inconsolable crying for 3+ hours that nothing helps
- Refusing feeds for 4+ hours
- Signs of dehydration: no wet diaper in 6+ hours, sunken soft spot, no tears when crying, dry mouth
- Bulging or markedly sunken soft spot (fontanelle)
- Unusual rash, especially with fever

## Call the pediatrician (after-hours nurse line) for

- Yellow tinge to skin or eyes appearing or worsening (jaundice — they'll triage it)
- Diarrhea that's persistent or has blood
- Coughing fits, wheezing, or snorty breathing that's new
- Eye redness or discharge
- Umbilical stump that smells, is red, or has pus
- Spit-up that's projectile, green, or has blood
- Anything that "feels off" — your gut counts as evidence
- Less than 6 wet diapers in 24 hours past day 5
- Weight loss or no weight gain by 2 weeks old

## Trust your gut

You know this baby better than anyone. If something feels wrong, call. The nurse line exists for exactly this. They'd rather hear from you 10 times for nothing than miss the one time it mattered.

## What NOT to do

- Don't give a baby under 6 months any over-the-counter cold medicine.
- Don't give honey under 12 months (botulism risk).
- Don't give water as a drink under 6 months unless your pediatrician says so.
- Don't take temperature in the mouth or armpit when accuracy matters — **rectal is the standard for under 3 months**.
- Don't wait it out on a fever in this age bracket. Call.
