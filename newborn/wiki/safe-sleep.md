# Safe sleep — the ABCs

> Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS) is rare but is the leading cause of death between 1 month and 1 year old. The single biggest factor you control is sleep environment. Three letters cover the essentials.

## A — Alone

Baby sleeps **alone** in their own sleep space. No co-sleeping in your bed, no sharing with siblings or pets. Room-sharing (baby in a bassinet next to your bed) is encouraged for at least the first 6 months — it actually *reduces* SIDS risk by up to 50%. Bed-sharing increases it.

If you're going to bed-share anyway (and many parents do), make it as safe as possible: firm mattress, no blanket or pillow near baby, no soft bedding, no alcohol or drugs in the parents' system, no smoking. But know the AAP recommendation is against it.

## B — Back

Baby sleeps **on their back**. Every time. Naps included. Until they can roll *both ways* on their own (typically 4–6 months), you place them on their back. Once they can roll both directions and have head control, you can let them sleep in the position they choose — but you still place them on their back to start.

Side sleeping is not a thing. Side becomes back or stomach almost immediately. Don't position with rolled blankets or wedges to keep them on their side — those are themselves a suffocation risk.

## C — Crib

Baby sleeps in a **crib, bassinet, or play yard** that meets current safety standards. Inside the sleep space:

- **Firm, flat mattress.** No memory foam, no in-bed loungers (DockATot, Boppy lounger, etc.) for sleep. Those are supervised-only.
- **Tight-fitting sheet** designed for that mattress. Nothing else.
- **Nothing else.** No blankets, no pillows, no bumpers (banned in the US since 2022), no stuffed animals, no positioners.
- **Sleep sack or wearable blanket** if you want warmth — never a loose blanket.

## A few more

- **Don't overheat.** One layer more than you're wearing is plenty. Overheating raises SIDS risk.
- **Pacifier at sleep onset** reduces SIDS risk. If it falls out after they're asleep, leave it. If breastfeeding, wait until breastfeeding is well established (~3–4 weeks) before introducing.
- **No smoking** in the home. Secondhand smoke is a major SIDS risk factor.
- **Vaccines on schedule** are associated with reduced SIDS risk.

## When the rules change

After 12 months, the strict ABC rules relax — toddlers can have a small lovey, a thin blanket, a pillow. But all of the first year, the answer to "should I add anything to the crib?" is no.
