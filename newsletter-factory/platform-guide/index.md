# 🧰 Platform Guide

Pick one platform. Don't bounce. Migrating costs subscribers (every time you change ESPs, you re-warm a domain and lose deliverability for 2–4 weeks).

## The decision matrix

| Your situation | Platform | Why |
|---|---|---|
| You want a built-in audience network and care about discovery | **[[substack|Substack]]** | The Substack network refers subs to you. Best free path to your first 1000 subs. |
| You're going paid + serious about growth tools | **[[beehiiv|Beehiiv]]** | Best-in-class growth features (referral programs, ad network, segmentation). |
| You're a marketer / creator who needs sequences and tagging | **[[convertkit|ConvertKit / Kit]]** | Most powerful automation + tagging. Built for creators with products to sell. |
| You want full ownership + custom domain + paid by default | **[[ghost|Ghost]]** | Open-source, self-hostable, paid memberships native, beautiful publishing. |
| You want minimal / dev-friendly + cheapest paid path | **[[buttondown|Buttondown]]** | Markdown-first, indie, cheap, no bloat. |

## The two questions to answer first

1. **Free or paid (now or later)?** All platforms support both, but the friction differs. Substack and Ghost are paid-first; Beehiiv and ConvertKit are growth-first.
2. **Do I want to own the platform or rent it?** Substack and Beehiiv are platforms (they own the brand surface). Ghost (self-hosted) and ConvertKit are tools (your brand sits on top).

Everything else is downstream.

## The single most important thing

**Send the first 10 issues consistently before you optimize the platform.** The platform doesn't matter at 0 subscribers. By issue 10 you'll know:
- Whether you can sustain the cadence
- Whether your audience is responding
- Whether your format is right
- Whether the platform is the bottleneck

Premature platform optimization is the #1 procrastination move. Substack with default settings will get you further than a perfectly-configured ConvertKit setup with no audience.
