# 📎 Buttondown

> Indie, markdown-first, dev-friendly, refreshingly minimal. Built and run by one person. The "no bloat" choice.

## Pick this if

- You want a dead-simple newsletter — write in markdown, hit send
- You're a developer or technical writer
- You want the cheapest paid path
- You don't need automation, ads, or marketplace networks

## Don't pick this if

- You want a discovery network (none here)
- You want best-in-class growth tools (Beehiiv beats it)
- You sell products through your newsletter (use ConvertKit)

## Pricing

- Free: up to 100 subs
- Tiered paid plans starting around $9/mo
- 0% revenue fee on paid newsletters (you only pay Stripe)

## Strengths

- **Markdown-native.** Write in markdown, including in your favorite editor. Send via API or web UI.
- **Honest pricing.** Cheapest way to get a custom domain + paid subs.
- **No dark patterns.** Indie maker, transparent roadmap, easy to leave.
- **Decent API.** If you want to integrate with anything custom, it's pleasant.
- **No surveillance.** Doesn't track readers' opens/clicks unless you opt in. Privacy-friendly.

## Weaknesses

- Smallest network of all the options listed.
- Templates are minimal — you'll style your own.
- Limited automation.
- Smaller community of writers.

## Recommended setup

1. Sign up. Connect a custom domain.
2. Use a markdown workflow you already know — VS Code, iA Writer, Obsidian.
3. Set up the welcome email (one email, simple).
4. Send weekly. Don't optimize the platform — write the issues.
5. Enable paid subs via Stripe when/if you need monetization.

## When Buttondown is the right choice

When the platform is the least interesting part of your newsletter operation. If you'd rather write 10 issues than configure 10 settings, Buttondown stays out of your way.
