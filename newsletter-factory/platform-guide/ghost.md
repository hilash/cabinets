# 👻 Ghost

> Open-source, self-hostable, designed from the ground up for paid memberships. The platform of choice for writers who want full ownership and a beautiful publishing surface.

## Pick this if

- You want full ownership of your platform, brand, and audience
- You publish to the web AND email — Ghost is great at both
- Paid subscriptions are the main monetization path
- You're comfortable self-hosting (or paying ~$9–25/mo for Ghost Pro)

## Don't pick this if

- You just want to send email and never deal with infrastructure
- You need complex tagging and automation (Ghost is intentionally simple)
- You want network-driven growth (Ghost has none — discovery is on you)

## Pricing

- **Self-hosted:** free (you pay for hosting — DigitalOcean, etc., ~$5/mo)
- **Ghost Pro:** $9/mo (Starter, 500 members) → $25/mo (Creator, 1k members) → $50/mo+ for higher tiers
- 0% revenue fee on paid memberships (you only pay Stripe's 3%)

## Strengths

- **Beautiful out of the box.** Themes are tasteful, fast, and modern. Better default look than any competitor.
- **SEO-first.** Web pages render properly, structured data, fast load times.
- **Native paid memberships.** Tier-based access, members-only content, free trials, all native.
- **You own everything.** Self-host and the data is yours forever.
- **API.** Robust API + webhooks if you want to integrate with anything.
- **Clean editor.** Block-based, fast, distraction-free.

## Weaknesses

- No discovery network — growth is 100% on your shoulders.
- Limited automation — basic welcome email, that's it.
- No referral program built in (3rd party integrations exist).
- Self-hosting requires basic server competency.

## Recommended setup

1. **Decide:** self-host vs Ghost Pro. Self-host saves money but costs time.
2. Set up at your custom domain.
3. Pick a theme. Customize sparingly — defaults are good.
4. Set up Stripe for paid memberships even if you're starting free (it's painless to enable later).
5. Configure tiers: free, paid, paid-yearly. Decide what's behind the paywall.
6. Send weekly issues + maintain a public archive (members-only for the most recent N or per-tier).

## Ghost vs everyone else

Ghost is the platform if you treat your newsletter like a publication, not a product. The publishing surface (the website, the archive) is a first-class citizen, not an afterthought. Other platforms send email; Ghost ships a magazine.

The trade-off is that Ghost gives you no help with growth. You'll need to drive subs yourself — through SEO, social, partnerships, search.
