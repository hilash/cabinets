# 📨 Substack

> The "you have an audience the moment you sign up" platform. Substack's recommendation network refers subscribers to you for free. The trade: you live on Substack's brand.

## Pick this if

- You want the fastest path to your first 1,000 subs and don't mind being on Substack's surface
- You write essays or curated content (the network favors that)
- You're okay with the 10% paid-revenue fee if/when you charge
- You don't need complex automation or heavy tagging

## Don't pick this if

- You want full ownership of your audience and brand surface
- You need automation sequences (welcome flow, drip campaigns)
- You sell products or have a serious commerce flywheel
- You publish on a topic Substack actively de-prioritizes

## Pricing

- Free to publish, including paid subscriptions
- 10% revenue fee on paid subs + Stripe fees (~3%) — all-in roughly 13%
- Custom domain support requires a one-time $50

## Strengths

- **Recommendations.** Other Substack writers can recommend your newsletter, and Substack actively pushes new subs to you. Single biggest growth lever for early-stage newsletters.
- **App.** The Substack app surfaces your work to readers — discovery you don't get on email-only platforms.
- **Notes.** Substack's Twitter-like feature drives traffic back to your issues.
- **Community comments.** Native comment threads under each issue.

## Weaknesses

- Substack-branded — every reader knows they're on Substack first, you second.
- Limited automation — no welcome sequence beyond a single "thanks for subscribing" email.
- No segmentation / tagging — everyone gets every issue.
- Basic referral program — much weaker than Beehiiv's.
- Migrations OUT are messy if you grow past it (you can export the list, but you lose the network and have to re-warm a new domain).

## Recommended setup

1. Sign up. Pick your URL.
2. Add a custom domain ($50) once you have 100+ subs — improves trust and lets you migrate later without re-emailing your list.
3. Write a short About page that's a "what to expect" + "who this is for".
4. Enable Recommendations from day one. Recommend other newsletters in your space — they're more likely to recommend back.
5. Post Notes 2–3x per week to drive Substack-internal discovery.
6. Pin your launch issue.
