# 🌱 Growth Guide

Newsletter growth is rarely about hacks. It's about: a sharp premise that resonates, consistency over time, and a small number of growth loops that compound.

## The four growth levers that actually work

### 1. Recommendations / cross-promo

The single most efficient lever for the first 1,000–5,000 subs. Other newsletters in your space recommend you, you recommend them.

- **Substack Recommendations** — native, free.
- **Beehiiv Boosts** — pay-per-sub, measurable.
- **Sparkloop** — pay-per-sub, integrates with most platforms.
- **Direct partnerships** — DM another writer in your niche, propose a swap. The most authentic.

Rule of thumb: aim to grow ~20–40% per month from recommendations in your first year. Below that, the premise isn't sharp enough; above that, you're growing fast and should keep doubling down.

### 2. Referral programs

Tier-based rewards for readers who refer friends. Beehiiv has the best built-in. Sparkloop / Letterloop work for other platforms.

Tiers that work:
- **5 referrals:** bonus issue, exclusive content, swag-light (sticker)
- **15 referrals:** a 1-on-1 reader call, behind-the-scenes archive
- **50 referrals:** physical swag (t-shirt, book), public shoutout
- **100 referrals:** lifetime free paid tier, custom thank-you

Don't launch a referral program before issue 10. You need substance to justify the rewards.

### 3. Search / SEO archive

If your platform produces SEO-friendly archive pages (Substack, Beehiiv, Ghost — yes; ConvertKit — no), every issue is also a piece of evergreen content. Title, structure, and internal-link your archive properly and you grow from search forever.

Quick wins:
- Use SEO-shaped titles in the archive (different from email subject) — "How to X" / "The 7 ways to Y"
- Internal-link past issues from new ones
- Add an alt-text version of every image
- Pin your best evergreen pieces to the home page

### 4. Cross-platform repurposing

Every issue should produce **3–5 social posts**:

- The strongest one-liner as a tweet / LinkedIn post
- A 280-char excerpt as a Note (Substack) or Tweet
- An infographic or screenshot of a key paragraph
- A clip / quote shared with permission tag (good for finding new audiences)

Repost your archive 1–2 times per quarter — most followers missed it the first time.

## Send-time optimization

| Day | Newsletter type that works |
|---|---|
| Monday | Strategy, planning, weekly digest |
| Tuesday | B2B, professional, work-context |
| Wednesday | High-engagement day, most newsletters |
| Thursday | Tech, deep-dives |
| Friday | Lighter content, lifestyle, weekend reads |
| Saturday | Long reads, indie / personal |
| Sunday | Long reads, life / philosophy / "Sunday paper" types |

Send time: **Tuesday/Wednesday/Thursday at 6–9am** in the recipient's timezone is the modal sweet spot for B2B. **Saturday/Sunday morning** for personal/lifestyle.

Don't optimize before issue 20. Send when YOU can sustain the cadence — that beats the perfect-time strategy.

## Deliverability basics

Bad deliverability = your issues land in spam = death by a thousand zero-opens.

- **Authenticate your domain.** SPF, DKIM, DMARC. Every platform walks you through this.
- **Warm up new domains.** Send to small batches of engaged subs first. Don't blast a cold list of 5,000 from a brand-new domain.
- **Clean your list.** Remove subscribers who haven't opened in 6 months. They drag down your engagement metrics, which drags down deliverability for everyone else.
- **Avoid spam triggers.** Already covered in [[../generators/subject-line-generator]].
- **Watch the bounce rate.** Above 2% is a problem. Above 5% is a deliverability emergency.

## Reply-driven growth

Replies are the leading indicator of a newsletter that compounds. They're also the most underrated growth lever.

- End every issue with one specific question.
- Actually reply to every reader. (Yes, every. While the list is small, this is your unfair advantage.)
- Quote (with permission) reader replies in future issues — readers feel seen.
- Track which issues drive the most replies. Make more of those.

A 1,000-sub newsletter with 50 replies per issue grows faster than a 10,000-sub newsletter with 0.

## What NOT to chase

- **Vanity subs:** giveaways, "win a $500 gift card to subscribe" — list bloats with disengaged readers, deliverability tanks.
- **Buying lists:** zero conversion, gets your domain blacklisted.
- **Generic SEO content farms:** writing for keywords with no voice produces no compounding growth.
- **Posting on every platform:** stretching across X, LinkedIn, Threads, TikTok, Insta diluteseffort. Pick 1–2 where your audience actually is.
