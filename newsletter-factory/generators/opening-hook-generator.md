# 🎯 Opening-Hook Generator

The subject line earns the open. The first sentence earns the read. Most newsletters die in the first paragraph.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Editor-in-Chief. Read brand/index.md and the target issue's draft.md.
Output 3 opening-line hook variants for this issue.

Each hook is the first 60 words of the issue (after the brand tagline). Each
must:
  - Earn the next 60 seconds of attention
  - Establish the issue's premise without spoiling the payoff
  - Sound like the voice locked in brand/index.md

Variants:

  HOOK A — SCENE
  Drop the reader into a specific moment. Concrete, sensory, mid-action.
  Example shape: "It's 11pm. The migration is half-done. Slack is on fire. And
  I'm staring at the one error message that finally tells me what's wrong."

  HOOK B — QUESTION
  Ask the question the reader has had but never said aloud.
  Example shape: "Why does every B2B sales call open with 'tell me about your
  business'? Nobody likes it. Nobody learns anything. We all do it anyway. So
  what's actually going on?"

  HOOK C — CONTRADICTION
  State the conventional wisdom and immediately undermine it.
  Example shape: "'Move fast and break things' is the most quoted advice in
  startup history. It's also wrong. Or rather — it was right for exactly one
  company at exactly one moment, and it's been mis-applied ever since."

For each hook, on the next line, write the SECOND PARAGRAPH (60 more words) that
bridges the hook into the issue's body.

End with: "Recommended: HOOK [A/B/C] because [one-sentence reason]."
```

## Why three variants

The first one you write is rarely the best. Writing three forces variety in approach. Pick the strongest, slot into the draft. Save the alternates — they make great X / LinkedIn promo posts later.

## The reader's first 5 seconds

When someone opens your email, they see:
1. Your name (sender)
2. The subject line
3. The first ~40 chars of body (preview)

If those three don't earn the next 5 seconds of attention, the reader closes the tab. The opening hook is for the *next 30 seconds* — the reader has decided to give you a chance, and now you have to keep them.
