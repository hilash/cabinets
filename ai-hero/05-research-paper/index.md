# 🔍 Stage 5 — Reading Some Research

**Time:** ~1 hour.
**Type:** Read + evaluate.

You've built the thing from the ground up. Now you need to be able to **read papers critically** — in every AI job, hobby, or follow-up project, you'll live on arXiv.

## The paper

[**arxiv.org/pdf/2501.00663**](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.00663) — a paper from Google Research.

Download it, open it, and actually read it. Not skim. *Read.*

## How to read a paper (when you're still learning)

The three-pass method (Keshav):

1. **Pass 1 — 5 min skim**: title, abstract, intro, section headings, conclusion. Goal: know what the paper *claims*.
2. **Pass 2 — 30 min**: read top to bottom, but don't panic over proofs or math you don't know. Look at figures carefully. Skip details you can't parse — mark them with a `?`.
3. **Pass 3 — if you need it**: re-read with full attention to the details you marked.

You will not understand every line. **That's the point of this stage.** You want to surface what you *don't* know, so you know what to study next.

## The question you must answer

> **Is this paper "useful"?** If you had a problem in this domain, is it a good choice?

Not "is it interesting." Not "is it technically correct." Useful. Would you reach for it?
