## The Ask Sequence

Work in this order. Each item is easier than the next.

### Step 1: Equity Clarity (before any counter)

Before negotiating anything, understand what the equity is actually worth. Use Script 4 from negotiation-scripts.md.

> "Sophie, I'm genuinely excited about this. Before I finalize my decision, could you help me understand the equity details? Specifically: the current 409A valuation, total shares outstanding, and vesting schedule? I want to model the realistic upside rather than just look at the number of shares."

**What to look for:**
- Strike price vs. current 409A (the gap = paper value)
- Total shares outstanding (to calculate your % ownership)
- Cliff (usually 1 year) and vesting period (usually 4 years)
- Whether options are ISO (US) or EMI (UK) or phantom equity (EU common)

**If equity is strong:** You have more room to settle on base.
**If equity is thin or opaque:** Base salary becomes even more important.

---

### Step 2: Base Salary Counter

Target: EUR 185–188K. Open at 188K. Settle at 185K.

> "Sophie, thank you for the offer — I'm really excited about the role and the team. After reviewing the full package and benchmarking against market data for Senior PM roles at AI companies of Hugging Face's scale, I was hoping we could discuss the base. Based on my experience in developer tools and the PLG motion I've run — including growing self-serve MRR 3x at DataPlatform — I believe a base of EUR 188K would better reflect the value I'd bring. Is there flexibility there?"

**If they push back and ask why EUR 188K:**
> "The posted range goes to EUR 190K, and I'm asking for just below that. I want to be fair — I'm not asking to exceed what you've indicated this role is worth."

**If they say EUR 175K is firm:**
Move to Step 3 (signing bonus). Don't argue the base further in the same conversation.

---

### Step 3: Signing Bonus

This is often the fastest lever. A one-time cost for them, meaningful for you.

> "Is there room for a signing bonus? I'm in a competitive process with another company that has a panel in a few days. A signing bonus — something in the EUR 10–15K range — would help me make a clear decision to commit here rather than waiting to see where that process lands."

**Why this framing works:** You're being transparent about the competing process, you're asking for a one-time cost (not recurring), and you're framing it as enabling a faster, committed yes.

---

**Critical:** Only use this if Anthropic actually moved you forward. Do not bluff.

### Step 5: Deadline Extension (if you need it)

If Anthropic is in-process and you need 3–5 more days:

**Most companies will grant 3–5 days if you ask warmly and with a specific date.** The ones who refuse are revealing something about their culture.
