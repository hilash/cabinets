# Negotiation Scripts

Adapted from the career-ops methodology. Every script is a starting point — personalize before using.

---

## Script 1: The Opening Counter

> "Thank you — I'm genuinely excited about this role and the team. After reviewing the full package and benchmarking against market data for [role] at [stage] companies in [location/remote], I was hoping we could discuss the base salary. Based on my experience with [specific proof point], I believe a base in the range of [target] would better reflect the value I'd bring. Is there flexibility there?"

**When to use:** First counter after receiving an offer. Keep the tone warm and grounded.

---

## Script 2: Geographic Discount Pushback

> "I appreciate the offer, but I want to flag that the geographic adjustment doesn't reflect the reality of the remote work I'd be doing. I'll be contributing the same output, with the same impact, to the same product — regardless of where I sit. I've seen [competitor] and [competitor] offering [range] for equivalent remote roles. Could we revisit the base to align with the role's scope rather than my zip code?"

**When to use:** When a company discounts remote comp based on location. Especially relevant for EU-based candidates at US companies.

---

## Script 3: Competing Offer Leverage

> "I want to be transparent — I have another offer from [company] that's at [total comp]. I'd prefer to join [your company] because [honest, specific reason — team, product, mission]. But I want to make sure the total comp is competitive enough that I don't look back and wonder. Can we close the gap?"

**When to use:** When you genuinely have a competing offer. Never bluff — it's a small industry.

---

## Script 4: Equity Deep Dive

> "I'm excited about the equity component. Could you help me understand: what's the current 409A valuation, how many total shares are outstanding, and what's the vesting schedule? I want to model the realistic value rather than just look at the share count."

**When to use:** For any startup offer with equity. The number of shares is meaningless without the denominator.

**Follow-up if equity seems thin:**
> "Given the stage and the risk involved, I'd like to discuss increasing the equity grant. I'm committed to a multi-year impact here, and I want the comp structure to reflect that commitment."

---

## Script 5: Signing Bonus Ask

> "Is there room for a signing bonus? I'm leaving [current situation — unvested equity, bonus timing, etc.] and a signing bonus would help bridge that transition. It's a one-time cost for you, but it meaningfully affects my ability to say yes quickly."

**When to use:** Signing bonuses are often the easiest lever to move. Always ask.

---

## Script 6: Deadline Extension

> "I'm very close to a decision, and I want to make a thoughtful choice rather than a rushed one. I have one other conversation that should resolve in the next [X days]. Could we extend the deadline to [specific date]? I don't want to accept and then second-guess — I want to be fully committed when I sign."

**When to use:** When you need more time for competing processes. Be specific about the ask.

---

## Negotiation Rules

1. **Never negotiate against yourself.** State your ask, then stop talking.
2. **Never lie about competing offers.** One verified lie ends the conversation.
3. **Always negotiate in writing after a verbal agreement.** "Can you send the updated terms in the offer letter?"
4. **The order matters:** Signing bonus (easiest) → Equity (medium) → Base salary (hardest) → Title → Perks
5. **Name the tradeoff, not just the ask.** "I'd accept today at X" is stronger than "I want more."
6. **Know your walk-away number.** Write it down before the conversation. Don't move it during.
