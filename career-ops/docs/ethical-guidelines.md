# Ethical Guidelines

Adapted from the career-ops project by Santiago Fernandez. Career Ops is a tool for empowered, honest job searching — not a system for gaming the process.

## Core Principles

### 1. Human-in-the-Loop
AI evaluates and recommends. You decide and submit. Career Ops never auto-submits applications, never sends messages on your behalf without review, and never takes action that represents you to an employer without your explicit approval.

### 2. Quality Over Quantity
This is a filter, not a firehose. The evaluation system exists to prevent spray-and-pray behavior. Applying to roles you're not qualified for wastes your time and the employer's time. Respect the 4.0 threshold.

### 3. Honesty in All Materials
- Never fabricate experience, skills, or achievements in CVs
- Never misrepresent your qualifications to pass ATS filters
- The CV Tailor reframes what exists — it never invents what doesn't
- If a skill gap is real, address it honestly in the cover letter or interview

### 4. Authentic Networking
- Outreach should be genuine, not transactional
- Don't send mass messages pretending to be personalized
- Offer value before asking for favors
- A warm connection is built, not manufactured

### 5. Respect Competing Offers
- Never fabricate or exaggerate competing offers during negotiation
- If you reference another offer, it must be real and verifiable
- The job market is smaller than you think — reputation compounds

### 6. Responsible Use of Automation
- Portal scanning finds opportunities. It does not spam applications.
- Batch evaluation scores roles. It does not mass-apply.
- Follow-up cadence suggests timing. It does not harass recruiters.

## What This System Is NOT For

- Mass-applying to hundreds of jobs with minimal review
- Generating fake or misleading resume content
- Automating applications to game ATS systems
- Impersonating the candidate in communications
- Circumventing application limits or processes

## Santiago's Original Note

> "I built career-ops because companies use AI to filter candidates, and I wanted candidates to have the same tools to filter companies. The goal is not to trick anyone — it's to make sure your time and the employer's time are both well spent."

— Santiago Fernandez de Valderrama, [@santifer](https://github.com/santifer)
