# Outreach

Networking and follow-up management. The `followup-cadence` job runs Tuesday and Thursday to draft messages and track timing.

## Follow-up Cadence Rules

| Situation | When | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Applied, no response | Day 7-10 | Polite check-in email |
| Applied, still no response | Day 14 | One final follow-up, then mark as ghosted |
| Screen completed, no update | Day 5 | "Checking in" message |
| Interview completed | Within 24h | Specific thank-you (not generic) |
| Networking outreach sent | Day 5 | Gentle bump (once only) |
| Positive response received | Immediately | Suggest next step to deepen |

## LinkedIn Outreach Templates

### Warm Reconnect
> Hey [Name] — saw [specific recent thing]. Congrats. I'm exploring [specific area] roles and would love your perspective on [one focused question]. 15 min?

### Cold-ish (Mutual Connection)
> Hey [Name] — [Mutual] and I worked together at [Company]. I'm a PM exploring [specific area] and noticed [specific thing about their work]. Would love to hear your take on [focused question] if you have 10 min.

### Post-Interview Thank You
> Hi [Name] — really enjoyed our conversation, especially [specific topic]. Your point about [specific detail] made me think about [genuine reflection]. Even more excited about [Company].

## Rules

1. Every message must be personalized — if it could be sent to anyone, don't send it
2. Under 100 words for LinkedIn, under 150 for email
3. Offer something before asking for something
4. One follow-up per person. If no response, move on gracefully
5. Keep the cadence sustainable: 3-5 messages per week, not 50 cold blasts
