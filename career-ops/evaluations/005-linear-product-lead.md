# Evaluation: Linear — Product Lead

**Overall Score: 3.87 / 5.0**
**Recommendation: Worth applying if pipeline is thin. Note the gaps.**

> Linear builds software for people who care deeply about the quality of their work. The company is opinionated, deliberate, and slow-by-design. If you want to ship fast and iterate scrappily, this is the wrong place.

---

## Block A — Role Summary (Score: 4.0)

**What is the actual job?**
"Product Lead" at Linear is a senior IC or first-line player-coach role. At a ~100-person company, this likely means owning a product surface end-to-end — probably the core issue tracker, integrations ecosystem, or a new platform area — while potentially coordinating with 1-2 other PMs. The title is deliberately flat (Linear avoids hierarchy inflation).

**Team & Reporting:**
- Small product org (~3-5 PMs total at Linear's scale)
- Reports to Head of Product or CPO directly
- Cross-functional pod model with strong engineering autonomy

**Growth Potential:**
Strong. Linear has grown from 20 to ~100 people and is scaling. A Product Lead who ships well here could become Head of Product within 2-3 years, or leave with serious DX credibility to a Series B/C company.

**Red Flags:**
- **Craft culture is non-negotiable**: Linear is famous for rejecting candidates whose work doesn't reflect extreme quality. If your portfolio shows speed over polish, this is a hard pass in interviews.
- **Slow by design**: Linear deliberately resists velocity pressure. If your proof points are "shipped X fast," reframe them as "shipped X right."
- **Ambiguous scope**: "Product Lead" without a defined domain suggests they're still figuring out what they need. Clarify in initial recruiter call.
- **Team-of-few risk**: At 100 people, you may be one of 2-3 PMs. Breadth is required; deep specialization is harder.

## Block F — Interview Prep (Score: 3.8)

**Likely Format:**
1. Recruiter screen (30 min — culture + basics)
2. Product critique: critique Linear itself (plan for this as a separate session)
3. Portfolio deep-dive with PM lead (60 min)
4. Cross-functional loop: 2-3 sessions with Engineering and Design
5. Possible take-home or async writing exercise

**5 Probable Questions:**

1. "Walk me through a product decision you're most proud of." → **Story 2: SDK v3.0** (shows craft + technical empathy)
2. "What would you change about Linear?" → Prepare a specific, well-reasoned critique with product judgment — not a wish list
3. "How do you decide what *not* to build?" → **Story 5: The Feature That Got Killed** (Jira deprecation — shows discipline)
4. "Tell me about a time you worked through disagreement with engineering." → **Story 4: The Migration Nobody Wanted**
5. "How do you think about developer onboarding?" → Activation work from Story 1, framed around product quality

**Company Intel to Weave In:**
- Linear's "Method" — their product philosophy blog is required reading before any interview
- They publicly reject roadmaps in favor of cycles — know this vocabulary
- Kalvari funding round, ~$35M Series B (2021) — still private, patient growth model

**Red Flag Questions to Ask:**
- "What does the PM team own vs. what does Engineering own?" (tests scope clarity)
- "How do PMs make decisions when there's no data?" (tests comfort with their anti-A/B-test culture)
- "What does success look like in the first 6 months?" (tests whether role is defined)
