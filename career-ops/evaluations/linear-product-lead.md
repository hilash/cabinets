## Block A — Role Summary (Score: 4.0 / 5.0)

**What is the actual job?**
Product Lead at Linear means owning the product direction for their core project management product — the one developers and engineers swear by. Linear is opinionated software: high craft, minimal bloat, zero tolerance for PMs who ship mediocre things. This is a player-coach role: setting product strategy while still being close to individual feature work.

**Team size / reporting:**
Linear has ~150 employees. Product team is likely 3–5 PMs. "Product Lead" almost certainly means managing 1–3 PMs while remaining an IC contributor. Reporting to VP Product or directly to founders.

**Growth potential (2–3 years):**
Product Lead → VP Product → CPO. At Linear's growth stage, this is a realistic arc. Linear could IPO or be acquired within 3 years — both create meaningful equity events.

**Red flags:**
- "Product Lead" is sometimes a senior IC title rather than a people-management role — clarify in recruiter screen. If it's IC-only, this under-delivers on the "targeting PM Lead/Head of Product" trajectory.
- Linear's product philosophy is strong and very specific. Cultural mismatch with PMs who like to move fast and ship broadly rather than slowly and precisely.
- EUR 140–175K ceiling. This is the lowest maximum in the current active pipeline (excluding Supabase).

## Block E — Personalization Tips (Score: 4.3 / 5.0)

**What makes this candidate uniquely good for Linear:**
1. **IC-to-PM arc** — Former engineer who crossed into PM is exactly Linear's target profile. They want PMs who can debate technical decisions, not just prioritize tickets.
2. **BuildTools CI/CD background** — Linear's core users are engineers running CI/CD pipelines. This background gives authentic empathy for the user base that most PMs lack.
3. **High-craft product track record** — SDK v3.0 with 96% developer satisfaction and the DX platform redesign are Linear-flavored achievements (opinionated, developer-first, ship-it-right).

**Culture signals from Linear:**
- Linear values writing over meetings (their Readme on this is public)
- They ship slowly and deliberately — not sprint-velocity-as-metric
- Strong opinions on product craft — PMs who ship sloppy things don't last

**Application angle:** Lead with the developer empathy story. Not "I built products for developers" but "I was the developer, then I built for them." The time-to-first-API-call proof point (45 min → 8 min) is viscerally relatable to Linear's users.

**Emphasize:** Engineering background, DX craft, high-standards bar for output quality
**De-emphasize:** PLG metrics, MRR growth (Linear isn't in growth-hacking mode), anything that sounds like "moving fast"
