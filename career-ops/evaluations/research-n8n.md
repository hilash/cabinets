## Axis 1: AI/Tech Strategy

**Core product:** n8n is a workflow automation platform — a "fair-code" licensed alternative to Zapier/Make.com that can be self-hosted. It allows technical users to build complex multi-step automations with 400+ integrations, custom JavaScript/Python nodes, and a visual workflow editor.

**AI direction (accelerating):**
- n8n AI nodes launched — native LLM integration (OpenAI, Anthropic, HF, etc.) directly in workflows
- AI Agent nodes — LLM-powered decision-making within automation flows (significant product bet)
- n8n is positioning as "the automation layer for AI workflows" — workflows that include LLM calls, vector DB lookups, and multi-agent orchestration
- According to their blog: AI template library growing rapidly; strong community demand for AI workflow patterns

**Tech stack:**
- TypeScript (Node.js) for the core platform and editor UI
- Vue.js for the visual workflow editor
- SQLite or PostgreSQL for persistence (user's choice on self-hosted)
- Redis for queue mode (scale-out)
- Docker-first deployment model

**Open source:**
- GitHub: `n8n-io/n8n` — 45K+ stars, very active
- Fair-code license: source available but not fully open-source (no commercial use without license)
- Large community of node contributors and template builders
- n8n Cloud as the hosted/managed offering

## Axis 4: Probable Challenges

**Challenge 1: Fair-code tension**
The fair-code license is a competitive moat but also a community friction point. Some contributors and users push back on it not being "truly open source." Head of Product will inherit this debate.

**Challenge 2: AI-first pivot execution**
The bet on AI workflow automation is directionally right, but execution is hard: LLM reliability, token costs, and debugging AI workflows are all unsolved UX problems. This is the PM's primary strategic challenge.

**Challenge 3: Zapier/Make.com competition from above**
Zapier has 7M+ users and is replatforming for AI. Make.com is growing in Europe. n8n's technical-user advantage is real but the window to capture market share is limited.

**Challenge 4: Self-hosted vs. Cloud split**
The self-hosted community and cloud customers have very different needs. Head of Product must manage two product surfaces with different velocity and support requirements.

**Challenge 5: Scaling the product org**
This is likely a "first real Head of Product" hire — building PM process, roadmap discipline, and team structure from scratch while still shipping. High autonomy but also high ambiguity.

## Red Flags

1. **Fair-code license legal risk:** If the license interpretation changes or triggers a community fork, product strategy gets disrupted.
2. **Funding stage uncertainty:** Exact runway and current ARR are not public. For a Head of Product role, you should ask about runway and growth trajectory before accepting any offer.
3. **First PM leadership hire:** High autonomy is exciting but comes with "building without a net." No playbook, inherited tech debt, possibly limited PM headcount budget.
4. **Berlin-anchored culture:** Despite remote-friendliness, expect pressure to be in Berlin for key strategic moments. Evaluate against personal location preferences.
5. **CTO relationship dependency:** You'll report to or work alongside Max Weber closely. Chemistry matters enormously at this level — trust your onsite read.
