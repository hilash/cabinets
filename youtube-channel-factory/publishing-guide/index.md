# 🚀 Publishing Guide

YouTube is a recommendation engine. Hitting "publish" doesn't make it watch your video — the algorithm decides whether to show it. Your job is to feed the algorithm the right signals.

## How YouTube actually decides what to recommend

YouTube optimizes for **session watch time** — total minutes watched on the platform per user session. Two videos earn equal recommendation:

1. A 10-minute video that 50% of viewers watch fully (5 min average)
2. A 5-minute video that everybody watches fully (5 min average)

The algorithm doesn't care which one — it cares that your viewer stays on YouTube. Optimize for **watch time** + **CTR** + **session continuation** (do they watch another video after?).

## The publish-time checklist

- [ ] **Title** — under 60 chars, hook keyword front-loaded, mobile preview tested
- [ ] **Custom thumbnail** — uploaded, tested at 320×180px
- [ ] **Description** — first 150 chars are the search snippet (write them like ad copy). Include 1 paragraph on the video, then chapters, then links, then channel boilerplate.
- [ ] **Chapters** — timestamps in the description (`0:00 Intro` format) become clickable chapter markers and improve retention
- [ ] **Tags** — 5–10 relevant tags. Lower priority than they used to be but still help.
- [ ] **Category** — pick the most accurate one
- [ ] **Playlist** — add the video to a relevant playlist (auto-plays the next video, drives session time)
- [ ] **End-screen** — set up to link to a related video + subscribe (see [[../generators/end-screen-generator]])
- [ ] **Cards** — 1–2 mid-video cards linking to related content (don't overdo it)
- [ ] **Pinned comment** — posted within 1 hour of publish
- [ ] **Captions** — uploaded or auto-gen-then-proofread

## Title & description craft

**Title** lives in 4 places and each has different rules:
- Search results: first 30 chars matter most
- Mobile homepage: first 40 chars
- Suggested videos sidebar: first 40 chars (truncates aggressively)
- Notifications: first 30 chars

Front-load the hook. Save the cute wordplay for the second half.

**Description** structure:

```
[150-char hook — appears in search snippets, write like ad copy]

[1 paragraph: what the video is and what they'll learn]

🎯 In this video:
0:00 — Intro
1:23 — [Chapter 1 name]
3:45 — [Chapter 2 name]
...

🔗 Links from the video:
- [Resource]: [link]
- [Tool]: [link]

🎬 More from this channel:
- [Related video title]: [link]
- [Related video title]: [link]

🌐 Connect:
- Newsletter: [link]
- Twitter: [@handle]
- Website: [link]

#tag1 #tag2 #tag3
```

The first 150 characters appear in Google search results. Treat them like a meta description.

## Shorts → long-form pipeline

Every long-form video should produce **2–4 Shorts**:

1. The strongest 30-second moment from the long-form (test cold-open hook variants).
2. A "teaser" Short with the title's promise + "watch the full video".
3. A "behind the scenes" 30-sec from the shoot.
4. A best-line / quotable moment.

Shorts get 100× more reach than long-form on a new channel. They're the cheapest growth lever. Tools: CapCut, Opus Clip (paid AI), Descript.

## First-48-hour metrics

YouTube decides whether to push your video in the first 24–48 hours. The signals it watches:

| Signal | Healthy starting range |
|---|---|
| CTR (impressions → clicks) | 4–8% |
| Average view duration | 50%+ of video length |
| Session time (do they watch another video after?) | Trending up over the channel |
| Likes, comments, shares per view | Higher = better (relative to channel norm) |
| First-view retention curve | No cliff in the first 30 seconds |

If CTR is below 3% in the first 48 hours, the title or thumbnail is wrong. Update both — YouTube does NOT penalize you for changing them.

## Cross-platform distribution

| Platform | What to post |
|---|---|
| Twitter / X | The most quotable line + link to video |
| LinkedIn | Long-form text post with the key insight + link (works well for biz/tech channels) |
| Reddit | Relevant subreddit, native upload (NOT a link) — link drops get downvoted |
| Instagram Reels | Same vertical clip as YouTube Shorts |
| TikTok | Same vertical clip with TikTok-native captions |
| Newsletter | If you have one — embed the YouTube player + 1-paragraph context |

## What NOT to do

- **Don't buy views, subs, or engagement.** YouTube detects it; videos get demonetized or channels banned.
- **Don't use clickbait you can't deliver.** First-time viewers might click, but average view duration tanks and the algorithm stops pushing.
- **Don't sub-for-sub.** Inflates the count, kills your engagement rate, gets you nothing.
- **Don't post inconsistently.** The algorithm rewards predictable cadence — once a week beats four times in one week and nothing for a month.
- **Don't hide your face if you're a vlogger or talking-head channel.** Connection beats production.
