# 📷 Cameras

## When to upgrade

Stop reading gear reviews. Answer these instead:

1. Have you published 10 videos with your current camera? If no — don't upgrade yet.
2. Do you have a *specific* shot you can't get with your current camera? If no — don't upgrade.
3. Is your audio already great? If no — upgrade audio first, not camera.

## What to actually buy (by tier)

### Free / phone

Any iPhone 12 or newer. Any flagship Android in the last 4 years. Lock to 4K@30fps, manual exposure, manual focus. The camera in your pocket is genuinely fine for talking-head and vlog content.

### $300–600 — webcam or basic mirrorless

- **Logitech Brio 4K** — best webcam if you're doing screen-record + face-cam tutorials.
- **Sony ZV-1F** — fixed-lens vlog camera. Flip screen, autofocus that just works.
- **Used Sony A6000 / A6400** — buy used. APS-C sensor, real lens mount, photographic image quality.

### $800–1500 — purposeful mirrorless

- **Sony ZV-E10 II / Sony A6700** — vloggers' standard. Eye-AF that doesn't quit. Good for talking-head.
- **Fujifilm X-S20** — gorgeous color science out of camera. Less aggressive grading needed.
- **Canon R50** — beginner-friendly Canon ecosystem.

### $2K+ — full frame / cinema

- **Sony FX3 / FX30** — actual cinema cameras with vlog ergonomics. Overkill for 99% of channels.
- **Canon R6 Mark II** — best autofocus on the market.

## What to skip

- 6K and 8K. YouTube delivers at 1080p / 1440p / 4K. Shooting at 8K just slows your edit and fills your hard drive.
- Drones — only if drone shots are part of your channel concept (travel, real estate, sport).
- Action cameras (GoPro / Insta360) — only if you're shooting action. They suck for talking-head.

## Lens before body

If you're already on a mirrorless system, a fast prime lens (35mm, 50mm, 85mm equivalent at f/1.8 or wider) does more for your image than a new body. A $400 used prime + $600 used body beats a $1500 new body + kit lens.

## Used market

Sony, Fuji, Canon all hold value well. Used bodies are usually 60–70% of new price. **MPB.com**, **KEH**, and **Adorama Used** are reliable. eBay if you accept the risk. Local camera shops often have unadvertised used inventory.
