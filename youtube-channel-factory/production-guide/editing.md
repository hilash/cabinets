# ✂️ Editing

The editor you can finish a video in beats the editor with the most features. Pick the one your hands actually launch.

## The four real choices

| Editor | Cost | Pick this if |
|---|---|---|
| **CapCut** | Free + paid | Mobile-first, Shorts-heavy, fast turnaround, AI features (auto-captions, auto-cuts) |
| **DaVinci Resolve** | Free (Studio paid) | Best free editor on earth. You want pro color grading + audio mix in one tool. Steeper learning curve. |
| **Final Cut Pro** | $300 one-time, Mac only | Apple-silicon Mac + you want the fastest workflow money can buy. Magnetic timeline takes a week to click. |
| **Adobe Premiere Pro** | Subscription | Industry standard. Best plugins (Boris FX, Magic Bullet). You care about portability between studios. |
| **Descript** | Subscription | Talking-head heavy. Edit by deleting words from a transcript. Game-changer for podcasts and tutorials. |

## What "good editing" actually looks like

Most new YouTubers over-edit. The signs of over-editing:
- A cut every 1.5 seconds, even when no cut is needed
- Stock B-roll that doesn't match the script
- Sound effects on every transition
- Zooms / shakes on every emphasis

The signs of under-editing:
- Long monotone takes with breath sounds
- "Um" and "uh" left in
- 5 seconds of dead air before someone speaks
- No on-screen text reinforcing the words

**Aim for the middle.** Cut breath pauses and "ums". Add B-roll only when the script *needs* a visual. On-screen text for any number, name, or term you say. One sound effect per minute, maximum.

## Captions are non-negotiable

- **85% of mobile viewers watch with sound off.** No captions = lost video.
- Auto-generate captions in your editor or YouTube Studio.
- **Always proofread.** Auto-captions get names, technical terms, and punctuation wrong.
- Burned-in captions (always visible) for Shorts. Toggle-able captions (CC) for long-form.

## Workflow that ships

1. **Rough cut** — assemble the talking-head only, in script order. No B-roll, no music, no captions.
2. **Tighten** — remove "ums", long pauses, awkward starts. Cut anything that doesn't earn its time.
3. **B-roll pass** — add visuals where the script demands them.
4. **Sound** — add background music (low, -25 dB), sound effects sparingly.
5. **Captions** — auto-generate, then proofread.
6. **Color** — basic exposure + white balance. Don't grade until you have 50 videos under your belt.
7. **Export** — 1080p or 4K, H.264, ~30 Mbps for 4K.

The first three steps make 90% of the difference. Sound, captions, color are 10%. Don't get stuck on color grading episode 3.

## Time per video

A reasonable starting target: **3 hours of editing per 10 minutes of finished video.** That's 3:1 ratio for a beginner; 1:1 for an experienced editor; 6:1+ for a heavily-edited essay/doc.

If you're spending 10:1 on every video, you're either over-editing or your shoot was sloppy. Fix the shoot, not the edit.
