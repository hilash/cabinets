# 📷 Production Guide

Most new YouTubers spend $2K on gear before their channel has any viewers. Don't be them. **Audio is more important than camera. Lighting is more important than camera. Camera is third.**

## The four-tier matrix

| Tier | Budget | Camera | Audio | Lighting | Editor |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **$0 — phone tier** | $0 | iPhone 12+ or any modern Android (4K@30) | Phone mic + quiet room | Window light | CapCut (free) or DaVinci Resolve (free) |
| **~$300 — basic tier** | $300 | Phone OR Logitech Brio webcam ($150) | Samson Q2U USB mic ($70) | One softbox or LED panel ($80) | DaVinci Resolve (free) |
| **~$1,500 — mid tier** | $1,500 | Sony ZV-E10 or Fujifilm X-S20 ($800) + 16-50mm lens | Rode VideoMic NTG ($250) | 2 softboxes + key/fill ($300) | Premiere Pro or Final Cut Pro |
| **$5K+ — cinema** | $5,000+ | Sony FX3 / Canon R6 + cinema lens | Lavalier (Rode Wireless GO II) + boom | 3-point lighting + practicals | Premiere/Resolve Studio |

## What to upgrade first

Always in this order. Skip ahead only if a tier is *already* good enough.

1. **Audio.** Bad audio = bad video. Most viewers watch on a phone speaker — fix the audio before you touch anything else.
2. **Lighting.** Even the cheapest camera looks pro under good light. The most expensive camera looks bad under bad light. Fix lighting before camera.
3. **Camera.** Once 1 and 2 are good, upgrade the camera.
4. **Lens.** If you've upgraded camera, a good prime (35mm or 50mm equivalent, fast aperture) makes more difference than another camera body.

## Format-specific recommendations

| Format | Critical gear |
|---|---|
| Talking-head tutorial | Mic + lighting > camera. Webcam tier is fine. |
| Vlog | Camera with a flip-screen + on-camera mic + portable LED. Sony ZV-1F / ZV-E10 series purpose-built for this. |
| Video essay | Audio + B-roll. Camera doesn't matter for the talking-head; you'll cover most of it with archival/B-roll anyway. |
| Mini-doc | Audio + camera + lens. Lavalier mic for interviews. |
| Screen-record (tech, software, gaming) | Mic > everything else. OBS or ScreenFlow. No camera needed. |
| Shorts / vertical | Phone is genuinely fine. Stabilization is critical (gimbal or in-camera). |

## Editing software

| Editor | Free? | Strong points |
|---|---|---|
| **CapCut** | Free (with paywall) | Fast workflow, AI features, good for Shorts. Mobile + desktop. |
| **DaVinci Resolve** | Free (Studio version paid) | Best free editor on earth. Pro color grading, full audio mix. Steep learning curve. |
| **Final Cut Pro** | Paid (~$300, one-time, Mac-only) | Fastest workflow on Apple silicon. Magnetic timeline. |
| **Premiere Pro** | Subscription | Industry standard. Best plugin ecosystem. Adobe tax. |
| **Descript** | Subscription | Edit by editing the transcript. Game-changing for talking-head/podcast. |

## The single most common mistake

New YouTubers buy gear they haven't grown into. Then they spend their first 10 videos fighting their own setup instead of making content. Stay one tier *behind* what you can afford. Upgrade only when the current tier is genuinely the bottleneck.

If your first 10 videos all look amateurish but the *content* is good, you'll grow. If they look beautiful but the content is mediocre, you won't. Spend 90% of your time on content, 10% on gear. Not the other way around.
