# 🎙️ Audio

If you only fix one thing on your channel, fix audio. Viewers will tolerate a soft-focus shot. They will close the tab on muddy audio in under 5 seconds.

## The audio chain

Source → mic → cable / wireless → recorder / interface → file → editor

Each link can ruin the whole. Common failure points:

| Link | Common failure | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Source (the room) | Reverb / echo, fridge hum, AC noise, neighbors | Treat the room before buying gear |
| Mic | Wrong type for the situation (condenser in untreated room) | Use dynamic mics in untreated spaces |
| Cable / wireless | Loose XLR, RF interference, dead battery | Always run two recordings (lavalier + on-camera) |
| Recorder | Levels too hot (clipping) or too low (noise floor) | Peak at -12 to -6 dB. Adjust BEFORE the take. |
| Editor | Auto-compression killing dynamics | Use a noise gate + light compression + EQ |

## Mics by situation

| Situation | Mic | ~Price |
|---|---|---|
| Talking-head, untreated room | Dynamic USB — Samson Q2U / Audio-Technica ATR2100x | ~$70 |
| Talking-head, treated room | Condenser — Rode NT1 / sE Electronics X1 | ~$200 |
| On-camera (vlog) | Shotgun — Rode VideoMic NTG / Sennheiser MKE 400 | ~$150–250 |
| Wireless / interview | Lavalier — Rode Wireless GO II / DJI Mic | ~$300 |
| Boom (mini-doc, scripted) | Sennheiser MKH 416 + boom pole + windshield | $1000+ |

**The biggest upgrade you can make: dynamic mic + treated room.** It's how every podcast you respect sounds.

## Treating a room (without buying anything)

The cheapest acoustic treatment you can do, ranked by impact:

1. **Record in a closet with clothes in it.** Fabric absorbs reflections. This sounds like a real studio.
2. **Record under a duvet.** Looks ridiculous, sounds incredible.
3. **Record in a small room with a rug, curtains, and a sofa.** Soft surfaces absorb. Hard surfaces (windows, walls, hardwood) reflect.
4. **Move the mic closer to your mouth (4–8 inches).** Closer = more direct sound, less room.

Acoustic foam panels do almost nothing for the price. Skip.

## Free improvements in post

- **Noise reduction:** Audacity (free), Adobe Podcast (free, web-based, near-magic), DaVinci Resolve.
- **EQ:** roll off below 80Hz (cuts rumble), lift around 3–5kHz for clarity.
- **Compression:** light, 2:1 ratio, -12 dB threshold. Don't overcompress — you'll squash the life out.
- **Loudness target:** -16 LUFS for YouTube. Most editors have a normalization tool.

## The double-recording rule

Always record audio in two places at once:
- **Primary:** lav + recorder, or USB mic into computer
- **Backup:** the camera's onboard mic, or a phone running Voice Memos

Once a session, the primary will fail. The backup saves the whole shoot.
