# 🔚 End-screen + CTA Generator

The last 30 seconds drive subs, watch-time-on-channel, and external clicks. Most creators waste them. Don't.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Creative Director. Read brand/index.md and the target video's script.md.
Output:

[VERBAL CTA — final 15 seconds, word-for-word]
ONE ask only. Pick the right one for this video:
  - Subscribe (use only if the video genuinely converts viewers — usually a strong essay
    or unique-value tutorial)
  - Watch this related video [title] (best CTA for retention on the channel — pick a
    related video from content-calendar.csv that pays off this video's curiosity)
  - Comment with [specific question] (best for engagement velocity — only if you'll
    actually respond)
  - Visit [link] (best if there's a clear action — newsletter, free tool, course)

Never stack CTAs. ONE ask, said once, with confidence. The CTA must logically follow
from what the viewer just watched.

[END-SCREEN LAYOUT — 20 seconds, format YouTube end-screen]
- LEFT: linked element to a related video on the channel (specify which)
- RIGHT: linked element to "best for new viewers" video (the highest-retention video on
  the channel) OR subscribe element
- CENTER: subscribe element if not already used on the right
- During the end-screen: the host either signs off briefly (3–5 seconds) or stays silent
  with B-roll. Never talk over the cards — viewers can't read AND listen.

[PINNED COMMENT — 1–2 sentences]
The first comment under the video, pinned. Either:
  - Asks a specific question that drives engagement (mirroring the verbal CTA)
  - Adds a useful clarification or correction the script couldn't fit
  - Links to a free resource referenced in the video

The pinned comment is real estate. Don't waste it on "thanks for watching".
```

## Why this matters

- **Suggested-video traffic** is YouTube's biggest source for established channels. The end-screen tells YouTube which related video to push.
- **Watch time on channel** is the metric that gets you recommended. Sending viewers to another of your videos at the end is a compounding loop.
- **Pinned comments** sit at the top of the comments and get 5–10× more engagement than other comments. They drive total comment count, which is a recommendation signal.
