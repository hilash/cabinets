# 🎯 Title & Hook Generator

Title decides if they click. Hook (first 8 seconds) decides if they stay.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Creative Director. Read brand/index.md and the target video's row in
content-calendar.csv. Output two blocks:

[8 WORKING TITLES — under 60 chars each]
Mix four shapes:
  1. Promise titles — "I built a $400/mo SaaS in 9 days"
  2. Question titles — "Why does my code keep breaking?"
  3. Number titles — "5 mistakes new YouTubers make week one"
  4. Curiosity-gap titles — "The one camera setting nobody tells you about"

Front-load the hook keyword. Don't waste the first 30 chars (mobile truncates).
No clickbait we can't deliver — every promise must be paid in the video.

[3 COLD-OPEN HOOKS — 0:00–0:08, word-for-word]
- Hook A — SCENE: drop the viewer mid-action. Concrete, sensory, in motion.
- Hook B — QUESTION: ask the question they've had but never said aloud.
- Hook C — CONTRADICTION: "Everybody says X. X is wrong because…"

Each hook ends on a sentence that earns the next 60 seconds without spoiling the payoff.
Read aloud silently. If it sounds written, rewrite.
```

## Choosing a title

- **CTR rule of thumb:** 4–6% on a new channel is healthy; 8%+ is great; 10%+ is rare. If you go below 3%, the title or thumbnail is wrong (almost never the topic).
- **Specific over clever.** "I switched from $5K cinema gear to a $300 phone — here's what changed" beats "My gear journey".
- **Mobile-first.** 70%+ of YouTube views are on mobile. Test the title in YouTube's mobile preview before publishing.

## Choosing a hook

The viewer has the next-video card in peripheral vision and a thumb that's *already moving*. Your hook must:

1. Not be skippable in the first 3 seconds (no music, no logo, no "hey guys")
2. Pay off the title's promise before it teases the rest
3. End on a sentence that *requires* the next 60 seconds

## After publishing

Save the 3 hook variants. Cut the unused ones into 30-second Shorts to drive the long-form. Free promo, almost zero extra effort.
