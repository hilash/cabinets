# Omega-3 (EPA + DHA)

## Claim
Adults whose diet is low in fatty fish (fewer than ~2 servings/week) typically benefit from an EPA+DHA supplement. Typical maintenance dose 1–2 g/day combined EPA+DHA. Higher prescription doses (e.g. 4 g/day icosapent ethyl) are used for elevated triglycerides under physician direction.

## Mechanism
EPA and DHA are long-chain omega-3 fatty acids. They incorporate into cell membranes, influence eicosanoid signaling (tilting toward less inflammatory metabolites), and play roles in cardiovascular, brain, and ocular health. ALA (plant-source) converts poorly to EPA/DHA in humans, which is why marine sources matter.

## Evidence
- **AHA Science Advisory (2019, Skulas-Ray et al.) — Omega-3 Fatty Acids for the Management of Hypertriglyceridemia.** Supports 4 g/day EPA+DHA (prescription) for TG >500 mg/dL. https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIR.0000000000000709
- **REDUCE-IT trial (Bhatt et al. 2019, NEJM).** Icosapent ethyl 4 g/day reduced major adverse cardiovascular events in statin-treated patients with elevated TGs. Strong RCT evidence for this specific population and dose.
- **NIH Office of Dietary Supplements — Omega-3 Fact Sheet.** Overview of dietary sources, dosing, safety. https://ods.od.nih.gov/factsheets/Omega3FattyAcids-HealthProfessional/
- **Cochrane Review (Abdelhamid et al. 2020)** — general-population omega-3 supplementation shows small reduction in CVD mortality; effect is modest at lower supplemental doses.

## Parameters
- **Form:** Triglyceride (TG) or re-esterified TG forms tend to have higher bioavailability than ethyl ester (EE) for non-prescription products. Prescription icosapent ethyl is EE but dosed high.
- **Dose (general maintenance):** 1–2 g/day combined EPA+DHA.
- **Dose (elevated TGs, under physician):** up to 4 g/day.
- **Timing:** With a meal containing fat.
- **Storage:** Refrigerate after opening; oxidation degrades quality and creates burp-back.
- **Quality:** Look for IFOS or similar third-party testing for oxidation (TOTOX) and contaminants.

## Who this does NOT apply to
- Anyone on anticoagulants (warfarin, DOACs) or antiplatelets (dual-agent) — bleeding risk; coordinate with physician.
- Fish/shellfish allergy — use algae-derived DHA/EPA.
- Upcoming surgery — physicians often pause high-dose omega-3 pre-operatively.

## Interactions
- Anticoagulants / antiplatelets (additive bleeding risk at higher doses).
- Blood pressure medications (mild additive BP-lowering effect).
