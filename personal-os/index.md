# Personal OS

Your life has departments. Staff them.

Personal OS is a cabinet for one person (and their household), structured like a small company where each "department" is an area of life. Each child cabinet has its own agents, its own working files, and its own weekly rhythm. A **Chief of Staff** at the root writes one Sunday brief across all of them.

> **Open the [[daily-console/index|🧭 Daily Console]] first.** Full-screen dashboard pulling from all six life areas — today's agenda · this week's calendar · the numbers (net worth, sleep, habit streak, inbox) · six area cards · brief schedule · 12-month net-worth chart.

## Life Areas (Child Cabinets)

- `brain/` — your second brain. Reads your notes and writing. Surfaces what you're thinking about. Helps you write better.
- `family/` — the *people* side of the household. Kids, partner, school, birthdays, activities, babysitters.
- `home/` — the *building* side of the household. Bills, maintenance, warranties, repairs, groceries.
- `money/` — budget, subscriptions, taxes, net worth. Where the money went and where it's going.
- `health/` — your expert team. Coordinator, protocol scientist, longevity physician, dietitian, coach, pharmacist. Every recommendation cites a source.
- `play/` — habits, hobbies, tinkering. Where the fun work lives.

## Root Team

- **Chief of Staff** — synthesizes each life area into one Sunday brief. Flags what moved, what stalled, what needs you this week.

## First Week Setup

1. Put one real thing into each life area. A few notes into `brain/`. Your bills into `home/bills.md`. Your goals into `money/index.md`. Last labs into `health/labs/`. One habit into `play/habits.md`.
2. Let the Sunday brief run once. It'll show you what each department looks like when it's sparse — and what to fill in next.
3. Install a **specialty cabinet** next to Personal OS as needed: `keto-hq`, `marathon-hq`, `dnd-table-hq`, `homeschool-ops`, etc. They read from your Personal OS data but live as separate installs.

## Trust Layer

Every health recommendation links back to `health/sources/`. No ungrounded advice. If an agent can't find a source for a claim, it writes one or refuses the recommendation.
