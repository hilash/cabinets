# Brain

Your second brain. Point it at an existing notes vault (Obsidian, plain markdown, whatever) and it reads, links, synthesizes, and writes with you.

## What lives here

- `notes/` — drop your markdown here, or symlink your existing vault
- `writing/` — drafts, essays, outlines
- `reading/` — highlights, quotes, book notes
- `briefs/` — weekly synthesis output

## Team

- **Librarian** — indexes and links your notes. Finds the orphans. Spots the clusters.
- **Writing Partner** — drafts with you, edits in your voice, argues with your weak middles.

## Weekly rhythm

- **Friday night** — Librarian rebuilds the index, surfaces notes you haven't touched in 90+ days that cluster with recent ones.
- **Sunday morning** — Writing Partner writes one paragraph: "what you seem to be thinking about this week."

## First-use checklist

1. Drop or symlink ~20 notes into `notes/`.
2. Let the weekly synthesis run once.
3. Read the brief. It will usually surface a half-finished thought worth continuing.
