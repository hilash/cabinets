# 🎙️ Zencastr

> Audio-first remote recording with the same local-recording trick as Riverside — each participant's mic records on their own machine and uploads after the call. Think "Riverside without the video focus".

## Pick this if

- Audio-only show (or video is an afterthought, not a priority)
- You want studio-quality audio from remote guests
- You want a slightly cheaper alternative to Riverside
- You don't need 4K video or live-stream features

## Don't pick this if

- Your show is video-first (Riverside has better video tooling)
- You go live regularly (use StreamYard)
- You record only solo (just record locally — no platform needed)

## How Zencastr works

Same model as Riverside:

1. Create a recording session, get a link.
2. Send to guest. They join in-browser, no install.
3. Hit record. Each participant's audio is captured **locally on their machine**.
4. Files upload after the session ends. You download separated tracks.

## Setup checklist

- [ ] 24h ahead: tell guest to use Chrome, plug in headphones, find a quiet room
- [ ] 5-min test call before the real one
- [ ] Confirm levels — peaks at -6 to -3 dB, no clipping
- [ ] Disable notifications on both ends
- [ ] Run local backup audio recording on your end
- [ ] Don't close the tab until upload says "complete"

## What you get out

- One **WAV** or **MP3** per participant (separated)
- Optional auto-mixed combined track (don't use it — mix yourself)
- Optional video tracks if you enabled video (lower quality than Riverside)

## Zencastr-specific notes

- Free tier exists with limited hours and audio quality. Paid is around $20/month — verify current.
- **Live editing soundboard** — useful for solo hosts who want to drop in stingers / sound effects during recording.
- **VoIP backup track** — if the local recording fails, Zencastr keeps a network-recorded fallback. Riverside has the same thing but it's hidden — Zencastr is more upfront about it.
- **AI features** — auto-transcription, post-production cleanup. Convenient for shownotes drafts.

## When to pick Zencastr over Riverside

You're audio-only, you want to save ~$5/month, and you don't need 4K video repurposing. If those three things are true, Zencastr. Otherwise Riverside.

If neither matters and you're starting fresh, just pick whichever onboarding flow you find less annoying — both will record your podcast at studio quality.
