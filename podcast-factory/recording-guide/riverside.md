# 🎬 Riverside

> Best-in-class for remote interviews where you want studio-quality audio AND video. The recordings are made locally on each guest's device, then uploaded — no network artifacts, even if the guest's wifi blips.

## Pick this if

- You record remote guests regularly
- You want video as well as audio (for YouTube, for clips)
- You want to be able to repurpose into Reels / Shorts / TikTok with vertical reframing
- You can live with $24/month or more (as of writing — verify current pricing)

## Don't pick this if

- You only ever record solo — overkill, just record locally
- You want to live-stream — Riverside has live-stream features but StreamYard is more mature for that
- You record in-person — Riverside is for remote setups; in-person you want a DAW + audio interface

## How a Riverside session works

1. You create a "studio" in the dashboard. You get a link.
2. You send the link to the guest. They open it in a browser (Chrome / Edge / Safari). No install.
3. The guest grants mic + camera permissions. They see themselves in a green-room view.
4. You hit record. Each participant's audio and video records **locally on their machine** at up to 48kHz / 4K.
5. After the call ends, each local recording uploads to Riverside in the background. You download separated tracks.

## Setup checklist before pressing record

- [ ] Tell the guest 24h ahead: "Please use Chrome, plug in headphones (not bluetooth), and find a quiet room."
- [ ] 5-minute test call before the real recording. Listen back to 30 seconds of their audio.
- [ ] Confirm the guest's mic level is hot but not clipping (peaks around -6 to -3 dB).
- [ ] Disable notifications on both ends — Slack, email, iMessage.
- [ ] Run a local backup recording on your end (QuickTime → New Audio Recording) just in case.
- [ ] Riverside auto-saves but does not auto-upload until the session ends — don't close the tab early.

## After recording

1. Wait until all participants' tracks have fully uploaded (the dashboard shows progress per participant).
2. Download the separated tracks (one per participant) — don't use the auto-mixed version, you want to balance levels yourself.
3. Edit in Descript, Riverside's own editor, Audition, or Logic.

## Riverside-specific gotchas

- **Bluetooth headphones add latency.** Wired only.
- **The guest's local file is the source of truth.** If their browser crashes after the call but before upload, the recording is on *their* hard drive and they need to manually upload it. Tell them this in advance.
- **Magic Editor / AI features** are useful for first drafts but always re-edit by ear — they cut breath patterns aggressively.
- **2-hour sessions max** in some plans. Long-form interviews, split into two sessions or upgrade.

## Riverside vs. SquadCast vs. Zencastr

All three do the same thing — local recording for remote guests. Pick based on which UI you find least friction. Riverside has the best-looking dashboard and best video. Zencastr is audio-first and slightly cheaper. SquadCast was acquired by Descript in 2023 and integrates beautifully if you edit in Descript.
