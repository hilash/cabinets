# ✉️ Vendor Outreach Generator

Vendors get hundreds of inquiries. The ones that get fast, useful replies are short, specific, and respectful of the vendor's time.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Planner. You are drafting a first-message email to a vendor.

Read `vision/index.md` for the wedding details. Then ask for (or read from
`vendors/tracker.csv`):
  - Vendor category (venue / catering / photographer / etc.)
  - Vendor name + business
  - Anything specific the couple liked about this vendor (a portfolio piece, a
    referral, a venue they've worked at)

Write a first message in this structure:

  [SUBJECT LINE]
  "[Vendor type] inquiry — [date] in [location]"
  Specific, scannable, professional.

  [GREETING]
  By name if known. "Hi [first name]," is fine — wedding industry is informal.

  [WHO YOU ARE — one sentence]
  "[Names], getting married on [date] in [location]." That's it.

  [WHY THEM — one sentence]
  Specific. "We saw your work on [referral source / Instagram post / wedding venue]
  and loved [specific element]." Generic praise reads as bot-spam.

  [THE ASK — one sentence]
  Are they available + what info would help them quote you. Don't ask for the price
  upfront — ask for availability + what they need from you.

  [THE LOGISTICS — bulleted]
    - Date: [date]
    - Location / venue: [if known]
    - Approximate guest count: [number]
    - Style / vibe: [3 words from vision/index.md]
    - Budget guidance (optional): [tier or range — only if you want to filter early]

  [CLOSE]
  "Would love to know if you're available. Happy to share more on a quick call if
  helpful." Don't apologize for the email or use "just checking in." Sign off
  warmly but briefly.

Output the message as paste-ready text. The user will personalize per vendor and send.
```

## What NOT to write

- "I want my wedding to be special and unique." — Every couple says this. It signals nothing.
- "Please send me your full pricing brochure." — Pushes the work onto the vendor and slows the reply.
- A 6-paragraph backstory of how you met, the proposal, etc. — they don't need it. Save it for the consult call.
- "I have a budget of X but I'm flexible." — They will absorb the X as the floor and quote at the top of your range.

## Following up

If you haven't heard back in **5 business days** (10 for popular categories like venue / photographer in peak season), one follow-up is appropriate:

> Hi [name], following up on the email below — wanted to make sure it didn't get lost. Are you available for [date]?

Two follow-ups is the cap. After that, they're not interested or not available — move on.

## Comparing quotes

Once you have 3 quotes for a category, compare on:

| Dimension | What to look for |
|---|---|
| Total cost | Apples-to-apples — match scope before comparing dollars |
| What's included | Service hours, deliverables, setup/breakdown, taxes, tips |
| Backup plan | What happens if they're sick / equipment fails / weather |
| References | Talk to 2 past couples — ask "what surprised you?" |
| Vibe | Did they "get" your vision in the consult? Worth ~10% premium for a yes |

Pick the one whose answers to those five hold up. Cheapest is rarely best, but most expensive isn't either.
