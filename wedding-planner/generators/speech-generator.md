# 🎤 Speech Generator

Best wedding speeches: **3 minutes**, **one specific story**, **end on the couple, not on yourself**.

Every other speech, no matter how heartfelt, has been the same speech. Specifics save you.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Planner. You are helping write a wedding speech.

First, ask the user for:
  - Role (best man / maid of honor / father of bride / mother of groom / couple /
    sibling / officiant / friend / "other")
  - Couple names
  - How long they've known the couple (or each side of the couple)
  - One specific story that captures something true — not "they're great together"
    but a moment with concrete detail (dialogue, sensory, place)
  - One thing about the partner that the writer didn't see coming and now appreciates
  - The "thing you'd never say at the toast" line — share it with the user FIRST so
    they can confirm it's safely off-limits (ex-relationships, financial issues,
    family drama, anything that would horrify the in-laws)

Now generate the speech:

[SPEECH — 3 minutes, ~450 words]

Structure:

  OPENING (15 seconds)
    State who you are and your relationship to the couple. Add ONE small joke about
    yourself — NEVER about the couple. Self-deprecation buys the room's trust.

  THE STORY (90 seconds)
    ONE specific story. Concrete details. Sensory. Time-and-place. The story should
    end with a line that reveals the partner's character — what makes them THEM.

  THE TURN (60 seconds)
    The bridge from "your friend" to "your friend who is now married". State what's
    surprised you about the partner. Or about the couple together. Earn the toast.

  THE TOAST (15 seconds)
    Land on the COUPLE, not on you. Address them directly. One short, specific
    promise or wish — not "may you have many happy years" but something tied to them.
    Raise the glass. Sit down.

[ALTERNATE OPENINGS — 3 variants]
Different first lines if the room energy is off and the writer needs to pivot.

[STAGE DIRECTIONS]
- Hold the mic 6 inches from your mouth. Don't dance with it.
- Pause after the toast for the room to drink. Don't fill the silence.
- If you cry, that's fine. Take a breath, take a sip, keep going.
- Read aloud at least 5 times before the rehearsal dinner.

[WORDS TO AVOID]
- "Without further ado"
- "When [partner] first told me about [partner]" (unless it leads somewhere new)
- Inside jokes only 3 people in the room will get
- ANY reference to past partners, exes, or "almost didn't happen" stories
- More than ONE poem or quote (and ideally zero — your specifics are stronger)

End with: "Length check: this draft runs ~3 minutes at conversational pace. If you
read fast, it'll be 2:30. If you cry, it'll be 4. Aim for 3."
```

## The 3-minute rule

Wedding speeches longer than 3 minutes are almost never better than the 3-minute version that was cut down. The room's attention has a curve — it peaks around minute 2 and drops sharply after minute 4.

If you're delivering more than 3 minutes, you have two stories. Pick the stronger one. Cut the other.

## Reading aloud

Read your speech aloud at least 5 times before the rehearsal dinner. Then read it once more, with a stopwatch. Then once more, in front of one trusted person who will tell you if a joke doesn't land.

That's the rehearsal. Your friends are forgiving but your speech doesn't need to depend on it.
