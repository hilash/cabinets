# 💍 Vow Generator

The vows you remember from real weddings have a structure: **a memory → a turning point → a promise**. Three minutes of writing time per draft, three drafts before you have the keeper.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Planner. You are helping write personal wedding vows.

First, ask the user (the partner who's writing them) for these inputs IF they are not
already in `vision/index.md` or in the prompt itself:
  - Partner's name (the one being addressed)
  - Three small, specific things you love about them — NOT generalities like "kind"
    but specifics like "the way you explain things three times if you think I didn't
    follow the first time" or "how you eat the burnt corner of every brownie so I
    don't have to"
  - One memory together that captures something true about who they are
  - One thing you've changed about yourself because of them
  - Two specific promises you can keep (not "I'll always make you happy" — that's
    not a promise, that's a wish)

Now generate three drafts:

[DRAFT A — 60 seconds, ~150 words]
The short version. Use the structure: ONE memory + ONE turning point + TWO promises.
Read aloud — must sound like the writer talks, not like a Hallmark card.

[DRAFT B — 90 seconds, ~225 words]
The standard version. ONE memory (with sensory detail) + the turning point + THREE
promises (one practical, one playful, one weighty).

[DRAFT C — 2 minutes, ~300 words]
The long version. Use only if the wedding is small or the writer is a strong reader.
Adds a second memory and one moment of acknowledged uncertainty ("I don't know
everything about you yet — but here's what I do know").

After all three:

[NOTES]
- Words to avoid (cliché): "soulmate", "completes me", "better half", "my everything",
  "from the moment I saw you" (unless literally true and specific).
- Words to embrace: actual proper nouns (places, foods, songs, dates), specifics,
  numbers, contradictions.
- Don't make jokes about marriage being long / hard / a prison. Save the jokes for
  the toast.

[WHICH ONE]
End with: "Recommended: DRAFT [A/B/C] because [reason]."
```

## Reading them aloud

Read each draft aloud to yourself before picking. Vows that read fine on the page often choke up in the actual ceremony — that's fine, but you want a draft that holds together emotionally even when you're crying.

## Coordination

If both partners are writing their own vows, coordinate **length and tone** in advance. One person delivering 4 minutes of poetry and the other delivering 30 seconds of "you're cool" creates an uncomfortable asymmetry. Aim for similar length and matching emotional weight.

You don't have to share drafts in advance — but agree on the rough shape.

## On the day

Print the final version on a single card. Put the card in a jacket pocket or with the officiant. Don't trust your phone to load at the altar.
