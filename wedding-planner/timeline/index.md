# 🗓️ Timeline

Wedding planning is the world's most expensive project-management exercise. The bootstrap generates the timeline backwards from your date. The Monday agent surfaces what's overdue and what's coming up.

## The standard 18-month timeline

> Scale to your remaining time. If you're 6 months out, compress. If you're 24 months out, you have breathing room.

### 12+ months out

- Set the budget (real numbers, not aspirational)
- Pick the date (or date range)
- Book the venue
- Book the photographer
- Book the officiant
- Book the videographer (if having one)
- Engagement party (if having one)

### 9–12 months out

- Book the caterer (if not venue-provided)
- Book the band / DJ
- Book the florist
- Engagement photos (if doing them)
- Save-the-dates designed and sent
- Bride: shop for dress (alterations take 3–6 months)
- Groom: shop for suit / tux
- Build the guest list (full version, not draft)

### 6–9 months out

- Book hair + makeup
- Book cake / dessert
- Book transportation
- Book hotel block for guests
- Wedding website live
- Order invitations (8–12 weeks lead time)
- Register (if doing one)
- Rehearsal dinner venue / plan

### 3–6 months out

- Send invitations (8 weeks before wedding for local, 10 for destination)
- Finalize ceremony (readings, music, structure)
- Finalize reception flow with planner / DJ
- Book honeymoon
- Start vow-writing process (drafts begin now)
- Marriage license research (varies by jurisdiction)
- Final dress fitting begins
- Order rings
- Final menu tasting

### 1–3 months out

- RSVPs in (3 weeks before wedding is the deadline)
- Final headcount to caterer + venue
- Seating chart
- Final payments to vendors per their schedules
- Day-of timeline finalized
- Vendor tip envelopes prepped
- Marriage license obtained
- Vows finalized
- Speeches finalized (best man, maid of honor, parents)
- Day-of emergency kit packed

### 1–2 weeks out

- Final headcount confirmation to all vendors
- Rehearsal scheduled
- Hair + makeup trials done
- Pack for wedding + honeymoon
- Pre-wedding family / wedding-party briefing (who needs to be where, when)
- Pay vendor balances per contracts
