## The day-of mindset

By the rehearsal, you've made every decision. Day-of, your only job is to be present. The runbook handles the rest.

A useful framing: **everything you forgot to plan happens the way it happens, and that becomes the wedding you actually have.** Most "disasters" — a delayed cake, the wrong song at the entrance, an aunt who didn't get the memo — are stories you'll laugh about later. Don't let them ruin the day in real time.

## What the couple does NOT do on the day

- Manage vendors
- Time anything
- Direct people
- Carry money or contracts
- Solve problems

That's what the day-of coordinator (or MC, or wedding-party point-person, or paid coordinator) is for. The couple's job is to stand next to each other, eat the food, dance, and remember the day.

## What the couple DOES do on the day

- Sleep the night before. Honest sleep, not "wedding eve adrenaline" sleep.
- Eat breakfast. Eat lunch. Drink water.
- Trust the runbook. If something goes off-plan, the team handles it.
- Take a 5-minute moment alone together at some point — usually right after the ceremony, before cocktail hour.
- Stay until the end. The send-off energy is unmatched.

## The 24-hour-before checklist

- [ ] Rehearsal complete — wedding party knows their cues
- [ ] Marriage license at the officiant's hand
- [ ] Vendor tip envelopes prepped, in one labeled bag, given to a designated tip-distributor (NOT the couple)
- [ ] Phone of every vendor saved in 3 places (phone, point-person's phone, paper)
- [ ] Emergency kit packed (see [[emergency-kit]])
- [ ] Final guest count locked
- [ ] Day-of contact list distributed
- [ ] Outfit + accessories laid out for morning
- [ ] Charging cables for phones at venue
- [ ] One trusted person designated to manage problems → they're the human escalation point
