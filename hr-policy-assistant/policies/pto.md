# PTO & Time Off Policy

**Effective:** January 1, 2026 · **Last reviewed:** April 15, 2026

## Summary

Full-time employees accrue 20 days of PTO per year (1.67 days/month). PTO can be taken in increments as small as 0.5 days. Up to 5 days may roll over to the following year; the rest are forfeited.

## Key rules

- Accrual begins on the first day of employment.
- PTO requests must be submitted at least 2 business days in advance for requests of 1–3 days, and at least 2 weeks in advance for requests of 4+ consecutive days.
- Managers can approve or deny requests based on team capacity.
- Unused PTO is not paid out on termination unless required by state law. See `policies/offboarding.md`.
- PTO does not accrue during unpaid leave.

## Edge cases

- **Parental leave:** PTO can be used to supplement parental leave pay. See `policies/parental-leave.md`.
- **Sick leave:** Separate sick-leave bucket of 5 days/year; does not roll over. Illness >3 consecutive days requires a doctor's note.
- **State-specific rules:** Employees in CA, CO, NY may have different accrual or payout rules. Contact HR for state-specific guidance.
