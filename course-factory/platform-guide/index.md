# 🧰 Platform Guide

Pick the platform that matches your **format** (cohort vs self-paced vs hybrid) and your **stage** (first course vs repeat creator). The wrong platform burns 20–40 hours of setup before you realize.

## The decision matrix

| Your situation | Platform | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Running a cohort, want community + live calls native | **Maven / Mighty Networks / School** | Cohort-native, scheduled, community-first |
| Self-paced + want fastest setup + lowest fees | **Teachable / Podia / Thinkific** | Course-hosting, simple, low overhead |
| Self-paced + you also sell products / coaching / memberships | **Kajabi** | All-in-one, expensive but covers everything |
| Want to keep students inside YOUR brand entirely | **Self-hosted (Notion + Stripe + Loom)** | $0 platform fees, full control, more setup work |
| You're a developer who wants markdown-source curriculum | **Self-hosted with custom build** | Full control, version-controlled, complex |

## What to NOT optimize for in your first course

New course creators waste weeks evaluating platforms. Two truths:

1. **Your first course will be on the wrong platform.** That's fine. You'll learn what you actually need by running it once.
2. **The platform doesn't make the course sell.** A great course on a mediocre platform sells. A mediocre course on Kajabi doesn't.

Pick the platform that gets you launching fastest. Migrate later if needed.
