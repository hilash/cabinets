# 🟦 Maven

> Cohort-native platform built for live, scheduled, group-paced courses. By far the strongest tool for cohort-based courses (CBCs).

## Pick this if

- Running a cohort with live calls, deadlines, and a community
- You want the platform to handle scheduling, attendance, recordings automatically
- You're targeting premium pricing ($1,500+) and need the production polish to justify it
- You're okay with revenue share (~10% of net) instead of monthly fee

## Don't pick this if

- Self-paced — Maven is overkill (and underbuilt for self-paced flows)
- You want a free / cheap path
- You need full custom branding — Maven's brand is visible

## Pricing

- No upfront cost
- Maven takes ~10% of revenue (verify current rate)
- They handle Stripe + tax + payment processing
- They actively help with marketing for high-quality courses (this is a real competitive advantage — they have an audience)

## What Maven does that nobody else does

- **Application gating.** Apply-to-join flows, with the ability to filter out wrong-fit students before they pay.
- **Cohort scheduling.** Calendars, time-zone handling, automated reminders.
- **Live session infrastructure.** Zoom integration with recording, transcripts, follow-up emails.
- **Discussion threads.** Per-cohort, per-session.
- **Student outcomes tracking.** Surveys, NPS, completion data.
- **Maven discovery.** Their site lists courses; potential students browse and discover.

## Trade-offs

- You're locked into Maven's brand surface — students know they're on Maven
- 10% revenue share adds up fast at scale
- Limited customization vs Teachable / Kajabi
- Self-paced replays of cohort recordings are okay but not the best self-paced experience

## When Maven is worth the 10%

You'll easily justify the 10% if any of these are true:
- This is your first cohort and you don't want to engineer infrastructure yourself
- You'll benefit from Maven's discovery / network of students
- You're charging premium prices ($2K+) — 10% on $2K is more affordable than building infra

If you're charging $300 for a cohort, Maven's 10% is $30 per student — but you might still come out ahead if Maven's network drives 30% of your enrollment.

## Recommended setup

1. Apply to teach on Maven (they curate; not all applications are accepted).
2. Once accepted, build the course outline in their system.
3. Set the cohort dates. Schedule live calls at recurring weekly times.
4. Set up the application flow — filter for fit, not just for pay.
5. Use Maven's email integration for cohort comms.
6. Plan the curriculum AROUND live sessions — Maven's value compounds when sessions are *the* learning, with self-paced material as preparation.

## Maven vs running a cohort yourself

You can absolutely run a cohort on Teachable + Zoom + Slack + a calendar tool. The trade-off:
- DIY: more setup, more flexibility, no revenue share
- Maven: less setup, less flexibility, 10% revenue share, plus their network

For first cohort: Maven if you can. For cohort 3+: consider DIY-ing once you know the playbook.
