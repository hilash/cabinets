# 🎯 School / Mighty Networks

Community-first platforms with course functionality bolted on. Pick one if the community is the core product and the course is the on-ramp.

## School

> Community + courses + leaderboard. Trending in the creator economy. Best-in-class for "course attached to a community" model.

### Pick School if

- The community IS the product, course is a hook
- You want gamification / leaderboard mechanics
- You want a single subscription to unlock community + course content
- You're targeting a US / online-creator audience

### Pricing

- ~$99/mo per community
- Per-member fees if charging access (verify current)

### Strengths

- Excellent community UX
- Built-in member apps (mobile)
- Leaderboards drive engagement
- Decent course-hosting attached
- Strong analytics

### Weaknesses

- Course-hosting is good, not great — Teachable is better at pure course UX
- One community per subscription
- Trends-driven culture (some ICPs hate the vibe)

## Mighty Networks

> Older, more enterprise-friendly community platform with course functionality.

### Pick Mighty if

- You want a community-first platform with white-label branding
- You're going to charge subscription for community access
- You want broader integration options than School

### Pricing

- Tiered, ~$33/mo (Community) → $179/mo (Business) → $360/mo+ (Path)

### Strengths

- Strong customization / white-label
- Cohort-style "Paths" feature (paid version)
- Mobile app per community
- Solid integrations

### Weaknesses

- Course-creation feels older than School
- UI is busier
- Not as "of-the-moment" — School / Circle have stolen mindshare

## When to pick community-first over course-first

| Question | Pick community-first if... |
|---|---|
| Will students need ongoing access after the course? | Yes |
| Does the value compound through peer-to-peer learning? | Yes |
| Are you charging recurring (subscription) vs one-time? | Recurring |
| Is your audience actively looking for "people like me"? | Yes |

If most answers are yes — School or Mighty. If your model is "buy the course, learn it, leave" — Teachable / Kajabi is simpler.

## The trap

Most new course creators think they want a community because it sounds nice. Real community management is a part-time job. If you're not committed to showing up daily for 6+ months, your community will die quietly and the course will sit alongside a graveyard of unanswered posts.

If unsure, launch the course on Teachable / Kajabi first. Add community in version 2 once you know whether students actually want it.
