# 📣 Launch-Sequence Generator

Most launches fail in two ways: cold list or weak sequence. The list is warmed in pre-launch (months ahead). The sequence is the 5–7 emails during launch week. Both matter. Don't focus on the sequence if the list is cold.

## Generator prompt

```
You are the Course Creator. Read brand/index.md (transformation, audience, price,
launch model).

Generate a 7-email launch-week sequence. Each email:

  [DAY 1 of 7 — OPEN]
  Subject: short, in the creator's voice. Often a question or contradiction.
  Send time: morning of the open day, ~6–9am recipient timezone.

  Body:
    - One specific story or insight (200–400 words) that ties to the transformation
    - Soft mention: "I've been quietly building [course name] — doors are open today"
    - Link to sales page (NOT a CTA button — a soft text link)
    - P.S. with one specific fact about the course (price, cohort date, modules)

  [DAY 2 — TEACH]
  Send 24h after Day 1.
  Subject: a teaching topic the course covers.
  Body: deliver a 400–600 word lesson on a specific topic from the curriculum. End with
  "this is a small piece of what we cover in [module name]." Link.

  [DAY 3 — STORY]
  Send 24h after Day 2.
  Subject: invokes a transformation story (the creator's or a past student's).
  Body: ONE specific story showing the State A → State B journey. Earn the price.
  Link with a clearer CTA: "I'd love to have you in the cohort if this resonates."

  [DAY 4 — OBJECTION]
  Send 24h after Day 3.
  Subject: states the objection plainly. ("Is this for beginners?" / "Will I have time?")
  Body: address ONE objection from sales-page-generator.md. Answer plainly. Link.

  [DAY 5 — PROOF]
  Send 24h after Day 4.
  Subject: a result-oriented line. ("3 things [past student] built in 4 weeks").
  Body: testimonials and concrete student wins. If first cohort, swap for "what you'll
  build" — concrete deliverables. Link.

  [DAY 6 — LAST CHANCE LIGHT]
  Send 24h after Day 5.
  Subject: notes the closing date without urgency-bait.
  Body: 100–200 words. The reasoning the creator believes. ONE more story. Hard CTA.

  [DAY 7 — CLOSE]
  Send the morning of the closing day.
  Subject: simple, direct ("Doors close at midnight" / "Last 12 hours").
  Body: very short — 80 words. State the close time explicitly. One CTA. No story.

After all 7, output:

  [POST-CLOSE]
  Day 8 — "Thank you to everyone who joined" + soft "if you missed the cohort, the
  wait-list for the next one is open" + link.
  Send to non-buyers + buyers separately if your platform allows.

Rules for ALL emails:
- Each email stands alone. The reader who only reads Day 5 should still get value.
- Do NOT use false urgency (countdown timers, "only 3 spots left" if not true).
- Plain text formatting beats HTML for solo creators. Looks less "marketing-y", higher
  open rates.
- Sign-off: the creator's name + one signature line they're comfortable with.
- Link the sales page in every email, but vary the CTA wording.
```

## Pre-launch (the 4–8 weeks BEFORE launch week)

Launch week is downstream of pre-launch. If the list isn't warmed, the launch sequence converts at 0.5%. If the list is warm, it converts at 3–8%.

Pre-launch checklist:
- [ ] Wait-list opt-in live on the sales page or a dedicated landing page
- [ ] 3+ pieces of pre-launch content released (newsletter / X / LinkedIn / YouTube) over 4–8 weeks, all teaching value FROM the course
- [ ] Wait-list specific bonus or early-bird offer
- [ ] Pre-launch survey to wait-list — what do they most want to learn? (Use answers to refine module 001)
- [ ] At least one "behind the scenes" piece — what you're building, why
- [ ] At least one personal-story piece — the journey that earned you the right to teach this

## Post-launch

After launch week, two parallel tracks:

1. **Deliver.** Teach the course at the planned pace. Update modules in real-time based on student questions.
2. **Document.** Capture every win, every "aha" moment, every testimonial. The next launch's sales page is built from these.

The first cohort generates the proof for the second. The second sells out faster than the first. The third is where most courses become real businesses.
