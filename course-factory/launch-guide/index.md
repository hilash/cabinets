# 🚀 Launch Guide

The launch is downstream of the pre-launch. The pre-launch is downstream of the audience. The audience is downstream of the consistent value you've delivered before the course existed.

## The three phases

### 1. Pre-launch (4–8 weeks before doors open)

Goal: warm the list. Build a wait-list. Generate qualified demand.

- [ ] Publish 3–6 pieces of content that teach value FROM the course (not the whole curriculum, but a slice). Each piece earns the right to mention the course.
- [ ] Set up the wait-list landing page with one specific opt-in.
- [ ] Send 1–2 wait-list-only updates per week — sneak peeks, behind-the-scenes, polls about content priorities.
- [ ] Run a wait-list-only "early bird" tier with a real reason (not just discount — better access, smaller cohort, bonus session).
- [ ] Cross-promote — guest posts on adjacent newsletters, podcast appearances, X threads.

Pre-launch artifact: `pre-launch-content.md` (filled in by the Monday agent during this phase).

### 2. Launch week (5–7 days)

Goal: convert the warm list. Run the email sequence. Be present everywhere your audience is.

- Run the 7-email launch sequence from `generators/launch-sequence-generator.md`.
- 3–5 social posts during launch week (one teaching, one story, one social proof, one objection-handler, one last-call).
- Live Q&A or AMA mid-launch (drives conversion of fence-sitters).
- DM warm leads who haven't bought (only if you have a real personal relationship — never cold).
- Be replyable. Every email reply gets answered within 4 hours.

Launch week artifacts: `launch-sequence.md`, `sales-page.md` (live by now), `objection-log.md` (track what students ask — fuel for next launch).

### 3. Post-launch (the 2–4 weeks after close)

Goal: deliver the course brilliantly. Collect proof. Set up the next launch.

- [ ] Welcome new students (personally if cohort is small, with a great onboarding email if larger).
- [ ] Deliver the course on the planned cadence.
- [ ] Document every "aha" moment, every win, every testimonial. This is gold for next launch.
- [ ] Run a mid-course pulse-check survey — what's working, what's not, what should change.
- [ ] After completion, run a NPS + testimonial survey. Ask for the win in the student's own words.
- [ ] Collect 5–10 strong testimonials. Update the sales page with real names + real photos (with permission).
- [ ] Plan the next launch — usually 8–12 weeks out, with a refined offer.

Post-launch artifacts: `student-wins.md`, `testimonials.md`, `next-launch-plan.md`.

## Launch metrics that matter

| Metric | Healthy first launch | Strong second launch |
|---|---|---|
| Wait-list size | 100–500 | 500–2,000 |
| Wait-list → buyer conversion | 3–8% | 6–12% |
| Sales-page → buyer conversion (cold) | 0.5–2% | 1–3% |
| Email open rate (launch sequence) | 35–50% | 40–55% |
| Refund rate | <5% | <3% |
| Course completion rate | 40–60% | 50–70% |

If wait-list conversion is below 3%, the offer or the price is wrong. If completion is below 30%, the course needs structural work — students aren't getting through it.
