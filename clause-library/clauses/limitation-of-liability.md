# Limitation of Liability

## Standard Language (First Paper)

> "IN NO EVENT SHALL EITHER PARTY BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, SPECIAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, EXEMPLARY, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO LOSS OF PROFITS, LOSS OF REVENUE, LOSS OF DATA, OR LOSS OF GOODWILL, WHETHER ARISING IN CONTRACT, TORT (INCLUDING NEGLIGENCE), OR OTHERWISE, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES. EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT SHALL NOT EXCEED THE TOTAL FEES PAID OR PAYABLE BY CUSTOMER TO VENDOR DURING THE TWELVE (12) MONTH PERIOD IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO SUCH LIABILITY."

**Risk Rating:** Low. Cap at 12-month fees, mutual exclusion of consequential damages, standard.

## Fallback 1 — Acceptable Compromise

> Expand the cap from 12 months to 24 months of fees. Retain mutual consequential-damages exclusion.

**Risk Rating:** Medium. Doubles our exposure ceiling. Accept only for high-value vendors where 12 months is demonstrably insufficient for remediation costs.

**Approval Required:** Senior Counsel.

**When to Use:** Counterparty is a high-criticality vendor (infrastructure, core SaaS platform) and the contract value is >€500k total. Do not accept for routine service providers.

## Fallback 2 — Walk-Away Line

> Carve-outs from the cap for: (a) breach of confidentiality; (b) IP indemnification obligations; (c) gross negligence or willful misconduct. Liability cap otherwise at 12 months.

**Risk Rating:** High. Carve-outs can result in effectively uncapped liability for IP and confidentiality breaches. Acceptable only if we have equivalent carve-outs on the other side.

**Approval Required:** General Counsel.

**When to Use:** Only if counterparty insists and the carve-outs are fully mutual. If they are not mutual — walk.

## When to Use This Clause

Every commercial agreement (MSA, SaaS subscription, professional services, data processing). Do not accept agreements without a liability cap.

## Negotiation Notes

**What counterparties push for:** Removal of the consequential-damages exclusion ("we need to recover lost profits if you breach"). Expansion of the cap to "total contract value" or "aggregate fees over the full term."

**Why we resist:** Consequential damages can exceed the contract value by orders of magnitude in a data breach or service failure scenario. Total-contract-value caps on multi-year agreements are effectively uncapped for long-term deals.

**What usually works:** Offer to expand from 12 to 24 months (Fallback 1) for Tier 1 vendors. Frame as "above industry standard." For the consequential-damages exclusion, offer carve-outs only for willful misconduct and gross negligence — not general negligence.

**Known acceptable carve-outs:** Death/personal injury, fraud, willful misconduct. These are standard and should be conceded early to signal reasonableness.
