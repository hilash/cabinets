# STAR Stories Bank

These are your go-to stories for behavioral interviews. Each one is tagged by the competency it demonstrates. Keep this bank warm — review it before every interview and add new stories as they come to mind.

> **STAR** = Situation, Task, Action, Result. Lead with the punchline (Result), then explain how you got there.

---

## 1. Killed a feature to save retention
**Tags:** prioritization, saying no, data-driven decisions

- **Situation:** At Acme SaaS, our most-requested feature (bulk export) was cannibalizing our sticky collaboration features. Users who exported stopped using the app.
- **Task:** I needed to decide whether to ship the v2 of bulk export or deprecate it entirely.
- **Action:** Ran cohort analysis showing export users had 40% lower 90-day retention. Proposed replacing bulk export with a collaboration-first sharing flow. Pitched the tradeoff to the exec team with data.
- **Result:** Week-4 retention improved by 12% in the quarter after deprecation. Support tickets spiked for 2 weeks then dropped below baseline.

**Best for:** "Tell me about a hard product decision" / "When did you say no to users?"

---

## 2. Shipped onboarding redesign in 3 weeks
**Tags:** speed, cross-functional leadership, activation

- **Situation:** Activation rate was 34% and dropping. The onboarding flow had 9 screens and asked for too much setup before showing value.
- **Task:** Redesign onboarding to get users to their first "aha" moment within 60 seconds.
- **Action:** Cut the flow from 9 screens to 3. Moved permissions and profile setup to after the first core action. Partnered with eng to build it in a 3-week sprint. Ran an A/B test with 50/50 traffic split.
- **Result:** Activation jumped from 34% to 52%. The simplified flow became the default and influenced the mobile app redesign.

**Best for:** "Tell me about shipping fast" / "Improving a key metric" / "Working with engineers"

---

## 3. Rebuilt the PM-engineering relationship after a failed launch
**Tags:** cross-functional trust, conflict resolution, process improvement

- **Situation:** A major feature launch at previous company went badly — missed deadline by 6 weeks, buggy release, blame on both sides. The PM-eng relationship was broken.
- **Task:** As the new PM on the team, I needed to rebuild trust and establish a working rhythm.
- **Action:** Started with 1:1s with every engineer. Learned they felt specs were thrown over the wall. Introduced collaborative spec reviews where eng shaped the solution, not just built it. Created a shared definition-of-done checklist.
- **Result:** Next three features shipped on time. Eng satisfaction survey went from 3.1 to 4.4 out of 5. The process was adopted by two other teams.

**Best for:** "Conflict resolution" / "Working with engineering" / "Turning around a bad situation"

---

## 4. Found the growth lever nobody was looking at
**Tags:** growth, data analysis, creative problem solving

- **Situation:** Growth team was focused on top-of-funnel acquisition, but CAC was rising and the CEO wanted answers.
- **Task:** Find a lower-cost growth lever that didn't require more ad spend.
- **Action:** Dug into the funnel data and found that users who invited one teammate in week 1 had 3x higher retention. Built a lightweight "invite teammate" nudge into the post-signup flow. Required zero new features — just a prompt at the right moment.
- **Result:** Organic invites increased 140%. Blended CAC dropped 22% over two months. The insight reshaped the team's entire growth model.

**Best for:** "Tell me about a growth win" / "Using data to find opportunity" / "Doing more with less"

---

## 5. Managed a P0 incident and the stakeholder communication around it
**Tags:** crisis management, communication, leadership under pressure

- **Situation:** Core API went down during a product demo for our biggest enterprise prospect. Sales, CS, and the CEO were all pinging within minutes.
- **Task:** Coordinate the technical response while keeping stakeholders informed without creating panic.
- **Action:** Set up a war room Slack channel. Pushed eng to focus on mitigation while I handled all stakeholder comms. Sent updates every 15 minutes with clear language: what's broken, what we're trying, ETA. Called the prospect directly to apologize and reschedule.
- **Result:** Service restored in 47 minutes. Prospect rescheduled and eventually signed. CEO later cited the incident response as a model for how to handle crises.

**Best for:** "Tell me about handling pressure" / "Communication skills" / "Leadership moment"

## How to Use These

- **Before an interview:** Pick 3-4 stories most relevant to the role and rehearse them out loud
- **During:** Don't recite. Hit Situation in 1 sentence, Task in 1 sentence, Action in 3-4 sentences, Result in 1-2 sentences with numbers
- **Adapting:** The same story can answer different questions with different emphasis. Story #2 works for "shipping fast" AND "improving metrics" depending on which part you highlight.
