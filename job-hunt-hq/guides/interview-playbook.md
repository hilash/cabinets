# Interview Playbook

Interviewing is a skill. Like any skill, it improves with deliberate practice and structured feedback.

## Interview Types

### Behavioral ("Tell me about a time when...")

**What they're testing:** Can you communicate clearly? Do you have real experience? Are you self-aware?

**Framework: STAR**
- **Situation:** 1 sentence of context
- **Task:** 1 sentence on what you needed to do
- **Action:** 3-4 sentences on what YOU specifically did (not your team)
- **Result:** 1-2 sentences with quantified outcome

**Pro tips:**
- Lead with the punchline: "I increased retention by 18% by killing our most-requested feature"
- Use "I" not "we" — they're hiring you, not your team
- Have a failure story ready that shows self-awareness and growth
- Silence is fine. It's better to think for 5 seconds than ramble for 2 minutes

### Product Sense ("How would you improve X?" / "Design Y for Z")

**What they're testing:** Can you think structurally about products? Do you start with users?

**Framework:**
1. **Clarify** — Ask 2-3 questions to narrow the scope
2. **Users** — Who are we designing for? Pick one segment
3. **Pain points** — What are their top 3 problems?
4. **Prioritize** — Pick the most impactful problem and explain why
5. **Solution** — Propose 2-3 solutions, evaluate tradeoffs, pick one
6. **Metrics** — How would you measure success?

**Pro tips:**
- Don't jump to solutions. Spend 40% of your time on the problem
- Say "I'd prioritize X because..." not just "I'd build X"
- Name the tradeoff explicitly: "We'd sacrifice A to gain B because..."

### Technical / System Design

**What they're testing:** Can you think about systems? Can you communicate with engineers?

**Framework:**
1. **Requirements** — Clarify functional and non-functional requirements
2. **High-level design** — Draw the boxes and arrows (3-4 main components)
3. **Deep dive** — Pick the most interesting component and go deeper
4. **Tradeoffs** — Discuss scalability, reliability, and cost tradeoffs

**Pro tips:**
- You don't need to be an engineer. You need to think about systems
- Name specific technologies only if you know them well enough to be challenged
- Ask about scale: "Are we designing for 1K users or 1M?"

### Culture Fit / Values

**What they're testing:** Will you thrive here? Are you self-aware? Are you a decent human?

**Common questions:**
- Why do you want to work here? (Have a specific, honest answer)
- What's your biggest weakness? (Name a real one and what you're doing about it)
- Tell me about a conflict with a colleague (Show resolution, not blame)
- Where do you see yourself in 5 years? (Ambitious but realistic)

## Before Every Interview Checklist

- [ ] Reviewed the company's product (used it if possible)
- [ ] Know the interviewer's name, title, and background
- [ ] Have 3-5 STAR stories loaded for likely questions
- [ ] Prepared 2-3 thoughtful questions to ask them
- [ ] Checked recent company news (last 30 days)
- [ ] Know the interview format and duration
- [ ] Tested your setup (camera, mic, quiet room) for video calls
- [ ] Have a glass of water nearby

## After Every Interview

Log it in `/interviews/feedback-log.csv`:
- What specifically went well
- What you would do differently
- The surprise question you weren't ready for
- Your honest vibe read (Strong / Warm / Neutral / Cold)
- Whether you sent a thank-you within 24 hours

## The Reps Strategy

Your first 2-3 interviews should NOT be at your top-choice companies. Use lower-priority opportunities to:
- Practice your STAR stories live
- Discover which questions trip you up
- Calibrate your energy and pacing
- Build confidence through real reps

Then bring your A-game to the companies that matter most.
