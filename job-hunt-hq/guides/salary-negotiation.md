# Salary Negotiation Guide

You earned the offer. Now earn the right compensation. Negotiation is not adversarial — it's a conversation about value alignment.

## The Mindset Shift

- They already decided they want you. The offer proves it. You have leverage.
- Negotiating does not make you greedy. It makes you a professional.
- The worst they can say is "this is our best offer." They will never rescind an offer because you asked.
- The difference between negotiating and not negotiating compounds over your entire career.

## Before You Get the Offer

- **Never share your current salary.** In many states it's illegal for them to ask.
- **Never name a number first** if you can avoid it. "I'm targeting competitive market rate for this role and level" is a complete answer.
- **If forced to give a range**, give a range where the bottom is your actual target. If you want $200K, say $200K-$230K.
- **Research the market.** Levels.fyi, Glassdoor, Blind, LinkedIn Salary, comp.fyi. Know the band.

## When the Offer Comes

1. **Thank them enthusiastically.** "I'm really excited about this. Thank you."
2. **Do NOT accept on the spot.** "I'd like to take a day to review the full package. Can you send the details in writing?"
3. **Get everything in writing.** Base salary, equity/RSUs, signing bonus, annual bonus target, benefits, start date.
4. **Ask about the equity.** Number of shares means nothing without: total shares outstanding, last 409A valuation, vesting schedule, cliff, exercise window.

## The Negotiation Conversation

### Opening

> "I'm very excited about this role and the team. After reviewing the package, I'd like to discuss a few things. Based on my research and the value I'd bring from [specific experience], I was hoping we could explore [specific number or range]."

### Key Phrases

- "Is there flexibility on..." — softer than "I want more"
- "Based on market data..." — grounds your ask in reality, not ego
- "What I'm most excited about is the role. I want to make sure the compensation reflects..." — shows it's not just about money
- "If we can get to X, I'm ready to sign today" — shows you're serious, not fishing

### What to Negotiate (in order of flexibility)

1. **Signing bonus** — often the easiest to increase. It's a one-time cost.
2. **Equity/RSUs** — more room here than base salary, especially at startups.
3. **Base salary** — often the hardest to move. Bands are real. But always ask.
4. **Start date** — want a week off between jobs? Ask for it.
5. **Title** — sometimes matters for your next job search. Costs them nothing.
6. **Remote flexibility** — increasingly negotiable. Worth asking.
7. **Annual bonus target** — sometimes adjustable at senior levels.
8. **Professional development budget** — conferences, courses, books.

## Competing Offers

The strongest negotiation position is having another offer. This is not about playing games — it's about having real options.

> "I want to be transparent — I have another offer at [Company] that's at [number]. I'd prefer to join [your company] because [honest reason], but I want to make sure the total comp is competitive."

**Rules:**
- Never lie about having another offer. If they ask for proof, you're stuck.
- Never use a fake offer to manufacture urgency. It's a small world.
- If you genuinely have competing offers, it's completely fair to share that.

## The Decision Framework

When comparing offers, don't just look at base salary. Score each on:

| Factor | Weight | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Total comp (base + equity + bonus) | High | Model equity at conservative valuation |
| Role and scope | High | Will you grow? Will you have impact? |
| Manager quality | High | Your manager is your job experience |
| Team and culture | Medium | Talk to future peers if possible |
| Growth trajectory | Medium | Where is the company going? |
| Remote / location | Medium | Affects your daily quality of life |
| Brand value for next job | Low | Famous company helps, but less than you think |

## After You Accept

- Get the final offer in writing before giving notice
- Send a warm thank you to everyone who helped (referrals, interviewers, recruiters)
- Update your pipeline — close out other applications with grace
- Celebrate. You did it.
