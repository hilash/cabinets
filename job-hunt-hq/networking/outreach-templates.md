# Outreach Templates

These are starting points, not scripts. Every message should be personalized. If it could be sent to anyone, it should be sent to no one.

---

## Warm Reconnect (Former Colleague)

> **Subject:** Quick question + congratulations on [recent thing]
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> Saw you [joined X / shipped Y / got promoted] — congrats, that's well-deserved.
>
> I'm exploring my next role and focusing on [specific area]. Given your experience at [Company], I'd love to pick your brain for 15 minutes about [specific question].
>
> No pressure if the timing's bad — I know how it goes. Either way, hope you're doing well.
>
> [Your name]

**When to use:** People you've worked with but haven't talked to in 3+ months.

---

## Referral Ask (Close Contact)

> **Subject:** Would you be up for connecting me with someone at [Company]?
>
> Hey [Name],
>
> I'm really interested in the [Role Title] at [Company] — [one sentence on why it's a great fit].
>
> I noticed [their contact] is on the team. Would you be comfortable making an intro? Happy to send you a blurb you can forward so it's low-effort on your end.
>
> Totally fine if it's awkward — I'd rather ask and hear no than miss a warm path.
>
> Thanks either way.

**When to use:** Strong relationships where you know someone at the target company.

---

## Cold-ish LinkedIn (Mutual Connection)

> Hey [Name] — we haven't met, but [Mutual] and I worked together at [Company] and they speak highly of you.
>
> I'm a PM exploring [specific area] roles and noticed [something specific about their work or company]. Would love to hear your perspective on [one focused question] if you have 10 minutes.
>
> No worries if not — appreciate you reading this far.

**When to use:** 2nd degree connections where you have a genuine shared context.

---

## Post-Interview Thank You

> **Subject:** Thanks for the conversation — [one specific reference]
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> Really enjoyed our conversation today, especially the discussion about [specific topic from the interview]. Your point about [specific thing they said] made me think about [your genuine reflection].
>
> I'm even more excited about [Company] after learning about [specific detail]. Looking forward to next steps.
>
> Best,
> [Your name]

**When to use:** Within 24 hours of every interview. Make it specific or don't send it.

---

## Gentle Follow-Up (No Response)

> Hey [Name] — just floating this back up in case it got buried. I know inboxes are brutal.
>
> [One-sentence reminder of what you asked.]
>
> If the timing's off, totally understand. Either way, appreciate you.

**When to use:** 5-7 days after initial outreach with no response. Send once. If no reply, let it go.

---

## Rules

1. Never send the same message to two people
2. Always reference something specific to them (recent post, shared experience, their work)
3. Keep it under 100 words for LinkedIn, under 150 for email
4. Don't apologize for reaching out — you're building a relationship, not imposing
5. Offer before you ask when possible
