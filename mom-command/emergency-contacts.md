# Emergency Contacts

> Print this. Stick it on the fridge. The brain doesn't work at 3am.

## When to call 911 (US) / 112 (EU) / your local emergency number

- Baby is not breathing or breathing very fast / hard
- Baby is unconscious or unresponsive
- Baby has a seizure
- Severe allergic reaction (face/lip swelling, hives + difficulty breathing)
- Severe injury, burns, or bleeding that won't stop
- **Under 3 months old: any rectal temperature ≥ 100.4°F (38°C) → ER, not pediatrician**

## Call your pediatrician (after-hours nurse line) for

- Fever ≥ 100.4°F (38°C) in babies 3 months or older
- Persistent vomiting or signs of dehydration (no wet diaper in 6+ hr)
- Diarrhea with blood
- Rash that's spreading or alongside fever
- Inconsolable crying lasting more than 2 hours
- Anything that "feels off" — your gut counts as evidence
