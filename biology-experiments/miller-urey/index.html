About

Miller-Urey experiments tested whether simple organic molecules could form from gases thought to resemble some early Earth atmosphere models when exposed to electrical discharge.

This simulator is illustrative rather than a quantitative kinetics model. Rates respond to limiting reagents, atmospheric balance, water vapor, and recent spark energy.

Glycine and alanine are easiest to form here; larger or more oxygen-rich amino acids need a stronger balanced mixture and repeated discharge.
