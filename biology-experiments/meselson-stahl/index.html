About

How to read this experiment

Meselson and Stahl grew bacteria in N15 so the DNA became denser than ordinary DNA. They then moved the cells to N14 medium and sampled DNA after successive rounds of replication.

  • Conservative replication would keep a heavy parental duplex and make a separate light duplex after one generation.
  • Dispersive replication would keep producing one mixed-density band that gradually shifts lighter.
  • Semiconservative replication predicts one hybrid band after generation 1, then a growing light band while the hybrid band halves each generation.

This page models the semiconservative rule directly: each daughter molecule retains one template strand and synthesizes one new N14 strand.
