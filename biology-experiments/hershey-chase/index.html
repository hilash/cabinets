About This Experiment

Hershey and Chase tested whether DNA or protein carried phage genetic information. They used T2 bacteriophages because a phage injects material into E. coli while most of its outer coat remains outside the cell.

32P Labels DNA, because DNA contains phosphorus.
35S Labels protein, because phage coat proteins contain sulfur.
Pellet Contains the infected E. coli cells after centrifugation.

After infection, the blender shears empty phage coats away from the bacterial surfaces. Centrifugation then separates the heavy infected cells into the pellet and leaves lighter phage coats in the supernatant.

When DNA is labeled, radioactivity follows the E. coli pellet. When protein is labeled, radioactivity mostly remains in the supernatant. The paired results support the conclusion that DNA is the genetic material.
