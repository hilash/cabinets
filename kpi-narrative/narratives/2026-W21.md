# KPI Narrative — Week 21 (May 18–24, 2026)

**Prepared by:** Metric Analyst + Narrative Writer
**Source:** `data/kpis.csv` · `fct_subscriptions`, `fct_signups`, `fct_activation_events`

---

## Executive Summary

Week 21 was the strongest growth week since the September launch. New signups jumped 14.6% week-over-week, almost certainly driven by the Product Hunt launch on May 18 — check UTM source in `fct_signups` to confirm. The Acme Corp upsell landed, pushing expansion MRR to $48K (+23% WoW), which lifted ARR to $4.82M. Activation rate climbed to 61.3%, the highest since tracking began, suggesting the onboarding A/B test variant is doing its job. Churn returned to trend at 2.1% after last week's brief spike. The one thing to watch: the signup surge will test activation at scale — if the rate holds above 60% through W22 and W23, the onboarding redesign is confirmed.

---

## Revenue

**ARR: $4.82M (+$69K WoW, +$340K vs 4 weeks ago)**

ARR crossed $4.8M for the first time. The driver was the Acme Corp upsell ($8K MRR, confirmed in `fct_subscriptions`) plus three smaller expansion events totalling $1.1K MRR. New logo ARR contribution was in line with prior weeks.

**MRR: $401,667 (+$5,750 WoW)**

MRR growth was slightly below the $6,500 WoW rate needed to hit the $430K MRR target by July. Watch: the Product Hunt cohort needs to convert to paid at ≥8% within 30 days to close this gap.

**Expansion MRR: $48,200 (+$9,100 WoW) ⚠ WARNING**

Above the 30-day mean by 1.7σ. The Acme upsell accounts for ~$8K of the spike; the remainder is organic seat expansion from two mid-market accounts. Expansion at this level is healthy — the warning flag is to confirm no billing error in `fct_subscriptions`.

---

## Growth

**New Signups: 1,847 (+235 WoW, +14.6%) ⚠ WARNING**

The Product Hunt launch drove a 14.6% signup spike, the largest single-week increase in 6 months. Historically, Product Hunt cohorts activate at ~45% vs the 61% baseline — the activation rate metric next week will confirm whether onboarding is absorbing the volume.

**Activation Rate: 61.3% (+3.5pp WoW)**

Best week on record. Variant B of the onboarding flow (introduced May 10 at 40% rollout) is the most likely driver — the 3.5pp lift is larger than prior onboarding changes produced. Recommend: accelerate the full rollout if W22 holds above 59%.

**Trial Starts: 623 (+82 WoW, +15.2%)**

In line with signup growth, as expected. Trial-to-paid conversion from the W20 cohort (who started trials 2 weeks ago) will be visible next week.

## Engagement

**DAU: 31,240 (+2,370 WoW, +8.2%)**

Daily active users hit their highest point since September 2025. The combination of new signups activating and existing users expanding usage drove the increase. No feature-specific breakdowns yet — run a cohort cut in Looker if you want attribution.

**Feature Adoption: 44.2% (+2.3pp WoW)**

Share of WAU engaging with at least one non-core feature crossed 44% for the first time. The Workflow Builder (launched April 28) is the primary driver based on `fct_feature_usage` event counts.

---

## Retention

**NRR: 114.2% (+0.4pp WoW)**

Net revenue retention is tracking above the 113% annual target. The Acme upsell contribution is the main driver this week. Structurally healthy; no action needed.

**Churn Rate: 2.1% (−0.3pp WoW)**

Back on trend after last week's 2.4% spike (which was two enterprise non-renewals). The 2.1% level is in line with the 30-day rolling mean of 2.5% — slightly better than average.

**Logo Retention: 97.8% (+0.2pp WoW)**

One account churned (Greenfield Co, $800 MRR, cited "price"). No enterprise logos lost.
