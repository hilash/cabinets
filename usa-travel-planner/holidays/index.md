## Federal holidays — the canonical place

| Holiday | Date | The right place to be | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New Year's Day** | Jan 1 | New Orleans, LA | Sugar Bowl + Bourbon Street + zero pretense |
| **MLK Day** | 3rd Mon of Jan | Atlanta, GA | The King Center + Ebenezer Baptist; the day matters here |
| **Presidents' Day** | 3rd Mon of Feb | Mount Vernon, VA | Wreath ceremony at Washington's tomb |
| **Memorial Day** | Last Mon of May | Arlington, VA | Flags-In ceremony at Arlington National Cemetery |
| **Juneteenth** | Jun 19 | Galveston, TX | Where Union troops announced emancipation in 1865 — the actual origin |
| **Independence Day** | Jul 4 | Bristol, RI | Oldest 4th of July parade in America (since 1785) — DC and Boston are great runners-up |
| **Labor Day** | 1st Mon of Sep | Chicago, IL | Jazz Festival + Grant Park; an authentic Labor-Day-as-Labor-Day vibe |
| **Columbus Day / Indigenous Peoples' Day** | 2nd Mon of Oct | Denver, CO or Berkeley, CA | The two cities that pioneered the Indigenous Peoples' Day shift |
| **Veterans Day** | Nov 11 | New York, NY | The largest Veterans Day parade in the country, since 1919 |
| **Thanksgiving** | 4th Thu of Nov | Plymouth, MA | The actual original site + reenactment villages |
| **Christmas** | Dec 25 | Bethlehem, PA | "Christmas City" — Moravian community since 1741 |

## 🎉 Cultural and regional holidays — also worth traveling for

| Holiday | When | Place | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mardi Gras** | Tuesday before Ash Wed | New Orleans, LA + Mobile, AL | NOLA is the party. Mobile actually started it (1703), and it's family-friendly |
| **St. Patrick's Day** | Mar 17 | Chicago, IL or Savannah, GA | Chicago dyes the river green; Savannah is the second-largest celebration in America |
| **Cinco de Mayo** | May 5 | Los Angeles, CA | Bigger than in Mexico (it's a regional Puebla holiday there) |
| **Pioneer Day** | Jul 24 | Salt Lake City, UT | Utah's biggest holiday — bigger than the actual 4th in some towns |
| **Halloween** | Oct 31 | Salem, MA or West Hollywood, CA | Salem leans into the witch thing for the whole month; WeHo throws the parade |
| **Día de los Muertos** | Nov 1-2 | San Antonio, TX or Olvera St, LA | Two of the most authentic Day of the Dead celebrations in the US |
| **Black Friday** | Day after Thanksgiving | Stay home | Or go shopping in Hudson Yards if you must |
| **New Year's Eve** | Dec 31 | New York, NY (Times Square) — or Key West, FL (drag-queen drop) — or Pittsburgh, PA (fireworks at the Point) | Times Square is the iconic but tourist trap; the others are authentic |

## 🎈 Celebrations that ARE the destination

These aren't traditional holidays but they're festivals that small American towns build their entire calendar around:

| Event | Where | When | Why this town |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Albuquerque International Balloon Fiesta** | Albuquerque, NM | First 9 days of October | 600+ hot-air balloons; mass ascension at dawn |
| **Daytona 500** | Daytona Beach, FL | February | NASCAR's Super Bowl; the town is the event |
| **Indy 500** | Indianapolis, IN | Memorial Day weekend | 300000 fans + milk + the bricks |
| **Kentucky Derby** | Louisville, KY | First Saturday of May | Two minutes of horse racing; a whole week of city-wide celebration |
| **Cherry Blossom Festival** | Washington, DC | Late March-early April | Tidal Basin in peak bloom + parade + kite festival |
| **Jonesborough Storytelling Festival** | Jonesborough, TN | Early October | A weekend of nothing but storytellers |
