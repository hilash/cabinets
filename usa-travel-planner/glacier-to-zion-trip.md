# Glacier → Zion Road Trip

A ~1,300 mi southbound park-hopper through the Northern Rockies, Greater Yellowstone, the Wasatch, and into Utah's red-rock country. Budget **10–12 days** to do it without burning out; tighten to 7 if you must.

## Route at a glance

| Day | From → To | Drive | Sleep | Park / focus |
|-----|-----------|-------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | Arrive Kalispell (FCA) → West Glacier | 0.5 h | West Glacier / Apgar | Settle in, sunset on Lake McDonald |
| 2 | Glacier NP — Going-to-the-Sun Rd | day-trip | West Glacier | Logan Pass, Hidden Lake, Highline if fit |
| 3 | Glacier — Many Glacier side | 2 h | Many Glacier | Grinnell Glacier or Iceberg Lake hike |
| 4 | Glacier → Bozeman / Big Sky | 6 h | Big Sky or Gardiner | Travel day, soak in Norris-area hot springs if time |
| 5 | → Yellowstone (north entrance) | 1 h | Canyon Village | Mammoth, Lamar Valley wildlife loop |
| 6 | Yellowstone — geyser basins | in-park | Canyon / Old Faithful | Grand Prismatic, Old Faithful, Midway |
| 7 | → Grand Teton → Jackson | 2.5 h | Jackson, WY | Jenny Lake, Mormon Row at golden hour |
| 8 | Jackson → Salt Lake City | 4.5 h | SLC | Reset day, laundry, gear, Antelope Island optional |
| 9 | SLC → Bryce Canyon | 4 h | Bryce / Tropic | Sunset Point, Navajo/Queens Garden loop |
| 10 | Bryce → Zion (east entrance) | 1.5 h | Springdale | Mt. Carmel Hwy, Canyon Overlook at sunset |
| 11 | Zion full day | in-park | Springdale | Angels Landing (permit) **or** The Narrows bottom-up |
| 12 | Zion → Las Vegas (LAS) fly home | 2.5 h | — | Buffer day for delays |

Total driving: **~1,300 mi / ~24 h behind the wheel**.

## Bookings to lock in now

- **Lodging inside parks** — Glacier (Many Glacier Hotel, Lake McDonald Lodge) and Yellowstone (Canyon, Old Faithful Inn) sell out 6–12 months ahead. Book first, plan around them.
- **Going-to-the-Sun Road vehicle reservation** — required in peak season (typically late-May through mid-Sept). Check nps.gov/glac for the current window.
- **Angels Landing permit (Zion)** — lottery via recreation.gov. Apply in the seasonal lottery *and* the day-before lottery as a backup.
- **Rental car** — one-way Kalispell → Las Vegas. Expect a one-way drop fee; book early for better rates.
- **Flights** — open-jaw: into **FCA (Kalispell)**, out of **LAS (Las Vegas)**.

## Park highlights

### Glacier NP (Days 1–3)
- **Must-do drive:** Going-to-the-Sun Road, east to west or vice versa. Start before 7 a.m. to beat the crowd and the reservation window.
- **Hike:** Highline Trail (Logan Pass → Granite Park Chalet, ~11.8 mi point-to-point with shuttle back) if you want the iconic ridge walk; Hidden Lake Overlook (~2.7 mi RT) for a shorter taste.
- **Many Glacier side:** Grinnell Glacier (~10 mi RT) or Iceberg Lake (~9.7 mi RT). Bear country — carry spray.

### Yellowstone (Days 5–6)
- **Lamar Valley at dawn** for wolves/bison.
- **Grand Prismatic Overlook** (Fairy Falls trailhead) — far better than the boardwalk view.
- **Geyser basin loop:** Old Faithful, Black Sand, Biscuit, Midway.

### Grand Teton (Day 7)
- **Jenny Lake → Hidden Falls → Inspiration Point** (~5 mi RT with the shuttle boat).
- **Mormon Row / T.A. Moulton Barn** at sunrise or sunset.

### Bryce Canyon (Day 9)
- **Navajo Loop + Queens Garden combo** (~3 mi) — the single best short hike in the park.
- **Sunrise at Sunrise Point** lives up to the name; **sunset at Sunset Point** does too.

### Zion (Days 10–11)
- **Angels Landing** (permit required, 5.4 mi RT, exposed chain section — not for vertigo).
- **The Narrows bottom-up** from the Temple of Sinawava (no permit; check flash-flood forecast). Rent neoprene socks + canyoneering shoes in Springdale.
- **Canyon Overlook Trail** (~1 mi RT) on the east side — huge payoff for the effort.

## Packing checklist (trip-specific)

- Layers: 40 °F mornings at Logan Pass; 95 °F afternoons in Zion.
- Rain shell — afternoon thunderstorms in Yellowstone/Tetons are a near-daily summer event.
- **Bear spray** for Glacier + Yellowstone (buy on arrival; can't fly with it).
- Sturdy trail shoes; water shoes if doing The Narrows.
- 3+ L water capacity per person — Zion + Bryce are dry and hot.
- America the Beautiful pass ($80) — pays for itself by park #3.
- Headlamp, paper maps, offline Google Maps regions (cell service is spotty across the whole route).
