## How They Pitch
Helios pitches enterprise-first: their slides open with a Fortune 500 logo wall and lead with compliance (SOC2 Type II, ISO 27001, FedRAMP In Process). They position as "the platform built for the enterprise procurement process" — meaning long sales cycles are a feature, not a bug. Their AI assistant is in beta (waitlist as of May 2026, no GA date).

## Our Counter
Velocity is our wedge against Helios. Their median enterprise implementation is 90 days (source: 3 G2 reviews + 1 Gong competitive loss call). Ours is 18 days. In the time it takes Helios to complete security review, we're live and the customer is getting value.

Lead with: "Helios is a great choice if you have 6 months. If your board needs results by Q3, let's talk about what 18 days vs. 90 days means for your team."

## Traps to Set
1. "What's Helios's committed implementation timeline in their contract — not the sales pitch?"
2. "Their AI assistant — is that in GA or still on a waitlist? When did they give you a GA date?"
3. "Have you spoken to any of their enterprise references that went live in under 60 days?"
4. "What's their implementation cost on top of the license — is that included?"
5. "How does their support SLA compare during the implementation window?"

## Proof Points `[UPDATED — 2026-06-12]`
- Two mid-market food companies cited the 18-day go-live as their primary reason for choosing us in the last 30 days — both had Northwind or Helios in evaluation. (Deal-tested: Meridian Foods + Summit Foods, May 2026.)
- A 600-person financial services firm chose us over Helios after Helios's implementation scoping call revealed a 90-day timeline. We went live in 19 days. (Deal-tested: Edgemont Capital referral, Q1 2026.)
- Helios's AI assistant is waitlist-only as of May 2026. Our AI feature has been in production since March 2025 — 14 months of customer feedback baked in. (Source: Helios blog, May 8 2026.)
- G2 review pattern (3 of 5 most recent): "support quality drops post-implementation"; "90-day onboarding promise became 5 months." (Source: G2 Helios reviews, April–May 2026.)

## Objection Responses `[UPDATED — 2026-06-12]`
- **"But Helios just announced an AI assistant too — aren't you the same?"** (3 Gong calls, week of May 18: Crestline, Solaris, Edgemont)
  Say: "Helios announced a waitlist. We shipped in March 2025 — 14 months in production, 340 customers using it daily. Would you buy a car that's been on a waitlist for 18 months?"

## Landmines to Avoid `[UPDATED — 2026-06-12]`
- **Expect "Helios has AI too."** Buyers are quoting their May 8 AI Assistant announcement. Don't get defensive — use the waitlist-vs-production response above.
- **Don't oversell AI.** We have it in production; they have a waitlist. Don't promise features outside the current release.
- **Don't dismiss their compliance story.** FedRAMP is real (even if In Process). If the customer needs FedRAMP, acknowledge it and check our own roadmap.
- **Don't attack their logo wall.** They have strong enterprise customers. Attack implementation time and AI velocity, not customer quality.
